3-Day Valpolicella and Soave Amarone Trail ★ 4.7

  1. Day 1

    Morning
    Depart Verona for Fumane in Valpolicella Classico (20 min); visit Allegrini estate for tasting of Valpolicella Classico, Ripasso and La Poja single-vineyard Corvina.
    Afternoon
    Drive to Negrar for an appointment at Quintarelli or Bertani for a historic Amarone vertical; book two to three months ahead for Quintarelli.
    Evening
    Return to Verona; pre-dinner aperitivo with Amarone at Enoteca Segreta or Osteria del Bugiardo.

  2. Day 2

    Morning
    Drive to Soave (25 min east of Verona); visit Pieropan estate for guided tasting of Soave Classico, Calvarino single-vineyard and Recioto di Soave; book well ahead.
    Afternoon
    Walk the Classico hillside vineyards to Gra' (Coffele estate) or visit Prà in Monteforte d'Alpone for their Monte Grande Soave Classico.
    Evening
    Drive to Mezzane di Sotto for early-evening tasting at Corte Sant'Alda biodynamic estate (appointment required).

  3. Day 3

    Morning
    Morning visit to Masi Agricola in Gargagnago di Sant'Ambrogio for the Ripasso history tour and a cellar walk through the appassimento fruttai drying lofts (seasonal availability September-December).
    Afternoon
    Final tasting stop at Bertani in Grezzana or Dal Forno Romano in Illasi (appointment essential, very limited availability) for prestige Amarone comparison.
    Evening
    Return to Verona; dinner at home base or departure.

2-Day Prosecco Hills UNESCO Road ★ 4.6

  1. Day 1

    Morning
    Arrive Valdobbiadene from Venice (1 hr) or Verona (1 hr 10 min); check in and walk the Cartizze hillside above San Pietro di Barbozza. Visit Ruggeri or Bisol for an introductory Prosecco Superiore tasting.
    Afternoon
    Drive the Strada del Prosecco south to Conegliano (30 min); stop at Nino Franco in Valdobbiadene for a Col Fondo tasting; visit the Scuola Enologica (wine school) in Conegliano for a historical overview.
    Evening
    Sunset aperitivo at a Valdobbiadene wine bar; try the Rive single-vineyard expressions from producers on the Strada.

  2. Day 2

    Morning
    Early morning walk on the UNESCO ciglioni terrace trails above Rolle di Cison di Valmarino. Visit Casa Coste Piane (by appointment) for col fondo Prosecco direct from the estate.
    Afternoon
    Drive to Costadila in Rolle for a tasting of their altitude-labelled col fondo expressions (appointment required). Continue to Cison di Valmarino village for the afternoon.
    Evening
    Return to base via the ridge road with views across the UNESCO landscape.

4-Day Veneto Grand Tour: Verona, Valpolicella, Soave, Prosecco Hills, Venice ★ 4.8

  1. Day 1

    Morning
    Arrive Verona; morning visit to the Arena and the wine-focused Enoteca Segreta for an introduction to Veneto wines by the glass.
    Afternoon
    Drive to Valpolicella (20 min) for tasting at Allegrini and Masi Agricola; focus on the Ripasso DOC and its appassimento technique.
    Evening
    Return to Verona for Amarone-focused dinner at Ristorante Il Desco or Locanda 4 Cuochi.

  2. Day 2

    Morning
    Drive to Soave (25 min); tasting at Pieropan for Soave Classico and Recioto di Soave DOCG; walk the hillside vineyard path.
    Afternoon
    Drive to Lake Garda (30 min west); tasting at Zenato in Peschiera del Garda for Lugana DOC and Amarone comparison.
    Evening
    Lakeside aperitivo in Garda town; Bardolino Chiaretto by the lake.

  3. Day 3

    Morning
    Drive north to Valdobbiadene (1 hr); walk the Cartizze sub-zone vineyards; tasting at Nino Franco for Prosecco Superiore range.
    Afternoon
    Drive the Strada del Prosecco east to Conegliano; stop at Ruggeri for Rive single-vineyard tasting; arrive in Treviso for the evening.
    Evening
    Aperitivo al Bacaro in Treviso piazza; try prosecco with cicchetti at a classic Treviso osteria.

  4. Day 4

    Morning
    Train from Treviso to Venice (30 min); morning walk through Rialto market and Cannaregio bacari.
    Afternoon
    Afternoon tasting at a Venice wine bar with Veneto focus; Enoteca Al Volto or Osteria da Carla.
    Evening
    Final evening in Venice; sunset on the Zattere with a glass of Prosecco Superiore.

Lake Garda Wine Weekend: Lugana, Bardolino and Custoza ★ 4.3

  1. Day 1

    Morning
    Arrive Garda town; morning check-in and walk the lakefront promenade. Drive south to Peschiera del Garda for a tasting at Zenato (Lugana San Benedetto and Riserva Sergio Zenato).
    Afternoon
    Drive to Custoza (15 min south of Garda) for a tasting at Cavalchina or Gorgo estate for Custoza DOC white (Garganega, Trebbianello, Fernanda blend).
    Evening
    Return to Garda town; aperitivo on Piazza Catullo with Chiaretto di Bardolino.

  2. Day 2

    Morning
    Drive north along the lake to Bardolino Classico zone; tasting at Guerrieri Rizzardi for Bardolino Classico and Chiaretto; the estate also produces a small Amarone.
    Afternoon
    Visit the Museo del Vino at the Zeni winery in Bardolino for a free self-guided tour of historical Bardolino winemaking equipment.
    Evening
    Final evening aperitivo at a Bardolino lakeshore wine bar.

3-Day Natural Wine Circuit: Gambellara, Soave Classico and VinNatur ★ 4.6

  1. Day 1

    Morning
    Arrive in Gambellara (40 min from Verona); visit La Biancara estate and meet Angiolino Maule; taste the Pico Gambellara skin-contact and the Sassaia Gambellara Classico. Advance appointment required.
    Afternoon
    Visit the VinNatur association premises in Montebello Vicentino; book a group tasting of VinNatur member wines to survey the Veneto natural spectrum.
    Evening
    Drive to Verona; aperitivo at Osteria del Bugiardo with a rotating natural wine pour.

  2. Day 2

    Morning
    Morning drive to Soave Classico (25 min); tasting at Filippi in Castelcerino for single-vineyard organic Garganega.
    Afternoon
    Visit Davide Spillare in Brognoligo di Monteforte d'Alpone for the Pico skin-contact Garganega (appointment required).
    Evening
    Drive to Vicenza (30 min); evening wine bar visit in Vicenza for regional-natural pours.

  3. Day 3

    Morning
    Drive to the Prosecco Hills (1 hr 10 min); visit Casa Coste Piane in Santo Stefano di Valdobbiadene for the benchmark col fondo (advance appointment essential).
    Afternoon
    Visit Costadila in Rolle di Cison di Valmarino for altitude-labelled col fondo comparison (appointment required).
    Evening
    Return to Verona or Venice for departure.

Itineraries in Veneto, FAQ

When is the best time to visit Veneto for wine?

Peak wine-travel season in Veneto is spring through autumn, with harvest the standout window.

Do I need an appointment to taste at Veneto estates?

classified-growth and grand-cru estates require booking days to weeks ahead; smaller family domaines often take walk-ins midweek.

What hours do Veneto cellars and tasting rooms keep?

most estates open 10:00 to 17:00 by appointment, often closed Sunday and Monday.

How does tipping work at Veneto tastings?

tipping is not expected at tastings; buying a bottle from the cellar door is the customary thank-you.

What is the one wine to try in Veneto?

Ask the next local you meet what they would order. Veneto rewards trust.
