Catarratto in Sicily

32 editor-picked estates growing Catarratto in Sicily.

Where to taste Catarratto in Sicily: the estates that grow it, how the region expresses the grape, and how to book a visit.

Catarratto estates in Sicily

Tasca d'Almerita ★ 4.7

Contea di Sclafani DOC, Etna DOC, Sicilia DOCNero d'Avola, Nerello Mascalese, Perricone, Inzolia, Catarratto, Grillo, GrecanicoFounded 1830Contea di Sclafani DOC

One of Sicily's oldest and most storied family estates, Tasca d'Almerita has farmed the Regaleali plateau since 1830. Their Rosso del Conte, a Nero d'Avola-led flagship, is among the island's most age-worthy reds.

Tasting: Estate visits and tastings at the historic Regaleali property in the heart of Sicily, bookable via the Tasca d'Almerita website.

Signature wines: tasca-dalmerita-rosso-del-conte, tasca-dalmerita-chardonnay, tasca-dalmerita-leone-dalmerita

Contrada Regaleali, 90020 Sclafani Bagni (PA), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Donnafugata ★ 4.7

Contessa Entellina DOC, Marsala DOC, Pantelleria DOC, Etna DOC, Sicilia DOCNero d'Avola, Syrah, Merlot, Cabernet Sauvignon, Grillo, Catarratto, Inzolia, Zibibbo, Grecanico, FrappatoFounded 1983Marsala DOC

Donnafugata, founded by the Rallo family in Marsala, has grown into a multi-estate Sicilian producer with vineyards in Contessa Entellina, Pantelleria and Etna, crafting wines from native varieties including the iconic Ben Rye passito.

Tasting: Daily guided tours and tastings at the historic Marsala cantina (10:00-18:00) and at the Contessa Entellina estate. Reservations required.

Signature wines: donnafugata-ben-rye, donnafugata-mille-e-una-notte, donnafugata-tancredi

Via Sebastiano Lipari 18, 91025 Marsala (TP), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Cantine Florio ★ 4.6

Marsala DOCGrillo, Catarratto, Inzolia, Nero d'AvolaFounded 1833Marsala DOC

Founded by Vincenzo Florio in 1833, Cantine Florio is the most iconic Marsala house in Sicily. Its historic tuff-stone cellars, with 104 arches and 1,400 barrels, are a landmark of western Sicilian wine history.

Tasting: Guided cellar tours of the historic 165-metre tuff-stone barrel chambers. Over 50,000 visitors annually. Bookable via the Cantine Florio website.

Signature wines: florio-targa-riserva, florio-morsi-di-luce

Via Vincenzo Florio 1, 91025 Marsala (TP), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Cantine Pellegrino ★ 4.4

Marsala DOC, Pantelleria DOCGrillo, Catarratto, Inzolia, Nero d'Avola, ZibibboFounded 1880Marsala DOC

Established in 1880, sixth-generation family-owned Cantine Pellegrino built its reputation on Marsala and Pantelleria passito wines. Its historic Marsala cellars are among the most visited in western Sicily.

Tasting: Wine tourism at the historic Marsala cellars via the Pellegrino Ouverture booking platform. Guided tours, tastings and food pairings.

Signature wines: pellegrino-marsala-superiore-riserva, pellegrino-nes-passito

Via Paolo Pellegrino 39, 91025 Marsala (TP), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Benanti Viticoltori ★ 4.8

Etna DOCNerello Mascalese, Nerello Cappuccio, Carricante, CatarrattoFounded 1734Etna DOC

One of Etna's pioneering estates, Benanti has been producing wine on the volcanic slopes since 1734. Their single-contrada whites from Milo are benchmarks for Etna Bianco Superiore, and their Nerello reds helped launch the modern Etna renaissance.

Tasting: Tuesday through Sunday by reservation. Three tasting programmes including Get to Know Etna, Contrade Selection and Library Vintages. Tours of the historic palmento and vineyard.

Signature wines: benanti-pietramarina, benanti-rovittello, benanti-serra-della-contessa

Via Giuseppe Garibaldi 361, 95029 Viagrande (CT), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Tenuta delle Terre Nere ★ 4.8

Etna DOCNerello Mascalese, Nerello Cappuccio, Carricante, CatarrattoFounded 2002Etna DOC

Founded in 2002 by Marc de Grazia on Etna's north slope at Randazzo, Tenuta delle Terre Nere was instrumental in establishing the single-contrada bottlings that made Etna internationally famous. Old-vine Nerello from plots like Calderara Sottana and Guardiola set the benchmark.

Tasting: By appointment, Monday to Friday. Contact via visite@tenutaterrenere.com.

Signature wines: terre-nere-calderara-sottana, terre-nere-guardiola, terre-nere-feudo-di-mezzo

Contrada Calderara sn, 95036 Randazzo (CT), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Graci ★ 4.7

Etna DOCNerello Mascalese, Nerello Cappuccio, Carricante, CatarrattoFounded 2004Etna DOC

Alberto Aiello Graci has been farming old-vine Nerello Mascalese and Carricante on Etna's north slope since 2004, producing single-contrada wines including the benchmark Arcuria Etna Rosso from 52 acres of ungrafted vines.

Tasting: Visits by appointment at the Passopisciaro estate. Contact via info@graci.eu.

Signature wines: graci-arcuria, graci-barbabecchi, graci-etna-bianco

Contrada Feudo di Mezzo, SP7iii, Passopisciaro, 95012 Castiglione di Sicilia (CT), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Pietradolce ★ 4.7

Etna DOCNerello Mascalese, Nerello Cappuccio, Carricante, CatarrattoFounded 2005Etna DOC

Founded in 2005 by Michele Faro at Solicchiata on Etna's north slope, Pietradolce works with old-vine Nerello Mascalese and Carricante across several contrade, producing one of the island's most age-worthy single-vineyard Biancos in the Archineri.

Tasting: Visits and tastings at the Solicchiata winery on Etna's north slope. Contact via the Pietradolce website.

Signature wines: pietradolce-archineri, pietradolce-etna-rosso, pietradolce-barbagalli

SS120 174, Contrada Rampante, Solicchiata, 95012 Castiglione di Sicilia (CT), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Girolamo Russo ★ 4.8

Etna DOCNerello Mascalese, Carricante, Catarratto, Inzolia, GrecanicoFounded 2005ORGANIC CERTIFIEDEtna DOC

Giuseppe Russo took over the family's old Nerello Mascalese vineyards in 2005 after his father Girolamo died. Fifteen hectares of organically farmed vines between 650 and 780 metres on Etna's north slope produce sought-after single-vineyard reds including San Lorenzo and a Rina.

Tasting: Vineyard visits and tastings with local cold cuts, cheeses and homemade bread. Contact info@girolamorusso.it or call 0942 983142.

Signature wines: russo-san-lorenzo, russo-a-rina, russo-feudo

Via Regina Margherita 78, 95012 Passopisciaro (CT), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Azienda Vinicola Calabretta ★ 4.7

Etna DOCNerello Mascalese, Nerello Cappuccio, CatarrattoFounded 1900Biodynamic PracticingORGANIC CERTIFIEDNaturalEtna DOC

Founded in 1900, Calabretta is one of Etna's oldest family wineries. The fourth generation farms 12.5 hectares of ungrafted old vines with copper and sulphur only, producing low-intervention wines from Nerello Mascalese that rank among the most authentic expressions of the north slope.

Tasting: Visits by appointment at the Randazzo estate. Contact via calabretta.net.

Signature wines: calabretta-gaio-gaio, calabretta-etna-rosso, calabretta-vigne-vecchie

Contrada Calderara, 95036 Randazzo (CT), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Murgo ★ 4.5

Etna DOCNerello Mascalese, Nerello Cappuccio, Carricante, CatarrattoFounded 1860Etna DOC

A historic estate on Etna's eastern slopes, Murgo introduced Sicily's most acclaimed traditional-method sparkling wine in 1990 using Nerello Mascalese. The 30-hectare property has been in the Scammacca del Murgo family since 1860.

Tasting: Wine tastings available Monday to Saturday 11:30 to 14:00. Restaurant and accommodation on site.

Signature wines: murgo-brut-metodo-classico, murgo-etna-rosso, murgo-etna-bianco

Via Zafferana 13, 95010 Santa Venerina (CT), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Frank Cornelissen ★ 4.9

Etna DOC, IGP Terre SicilianeNerello Mascalese, Nerello Cappuccio, Catarratto, Minella, CarricanteFounded 2001Biodynamic PracticingORGANIC CERTIFIEDNaturalEtna DOC

Belgian natural-wine pioneer Frank Cornelissen established his estate on Etna's north slope in 2001, working without sulphur at all. His Magma cuvee from ungrafted pre-phylloxera Nerello vines above 1,000 metres is among Sicily's most sought-after cult wines.

Tasting: Five tasting experiences from the Classic (5 wines, EU40) to the Magma private (6 crus including Grand Vin Magma, EU1,400 for up to 6 people). E-bike vineyard tours available. Book via pr@frankcornelissen.it.

Signature wines: cornelissen-magma, cornelissen-munjebel-rosso, cornelissen-munjebel-bianco

Via Canonico Zumbo 1, Passopisciaro, 95012 Castiglione di Sicilia (CT), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Feudo Montoni ★ 4.7

Sicilia DOCNero d'Avola, Catarratto, GrilloORGANIC CERTIFIEDSicilia DOC

Fabio Sireci's Feudo Montoni sits in the hills of central Sicily at Cammarata, farming pre-phylloxera Nero d'Avola vines. The Vrucara single-vineyard bottling from ungrafted vines is one of Sicily's most distinctive and sought-after Nero d'Avola expressions.

Tasting: Visits by appointment at the Cammarata estate in central Sicily. Contact info@feudomontoni.it.

Signature wines: feudo-montoni-vrucara, feudo-montoni-lagnusa, feudo-montoni-grillo-della-timpa

Contrada Montoni Vecchi, 92022 Cammarata (AG), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Firriato ★ 4.3

Etna DOC, Sicilia DOCNero d'Avola, Nerello Mascalese, Perricone, Syrah, Cabernet Sauvignon, Grillo, Catarratto, Chardonnay, InzoliaFounded 1978Sicilia DOC

Founded by Salvatore Di Gaetano in 1978, Firriato has grown into one of Sicily's largest and most diverse producers with approximately 490 hectares across seven estates. Their reach spans from the Trapani hills to Etna and the island of Favignana.

Tasting: Guided cellar tours, vineyard visits and tastings at the Baglio Soria resort in Paceco and at Cavanera Etnea on Etna. By appointment.

Signature wines: firriato-camelot, firriato-ribeca, firriato-chiaramonte-nero-davola

Via Trapani 4, 91027 Paceco (TP), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Baglio di Pianetto ★ 4.5

Sicilia DOCNero d'Avola, Syrah, Merlot, Cabernet Sauvignon, Inzolia, Catarratto, ViognierFounded 1997ORGANIC CERTIFIEDSicilia DOC

Founded in 1997 by Count Paolo Marzotto, Baglio di Pianetto sits at 900 metres near Santa Cristina Gela, 25 kilometres from Palermo. The estate blends Sicilian and French winemaking traditions across 106 hectares, with an elegant agrirelais for overnight stays.

Tasting: Private visits and introductory wine courses at the 20-room agrirelais. Monday to Friday 09:00 to 17:00 by appointment.

Signature wines: baglio-pianetto-ramione, baglio-pianetto-ficiligno, baglio-pianetto-viafrancia

Via Francia, 90030 Santa Cristina Gela (PA), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Palmento Costanzo ★ 4.6

Etna DOCNerello Mascalese, Nerello Cappuccio, Carricante, CatarrattoFounded 2011ORGANIC CERTIFIEDEtna DOC

Founded in 2011 by Mimmo and Valeria Costanzo, Palmento Costanzo farms approximately ten hectares organically on Etna's north slope in Contrada Santo Spirito. They produce contrada-specific wines with manual cultivation and a fully organic approach.

Tasting: Visite e Degustazioni bookable via the estate website. Contact info@palmentocostanzo.com or call +39 0942 983239.

Signature wines: palmento-costanzo-mofete-bianco, palmento-costanzo-mofete-rosso, palmento-costanzo-di-me

Contrada Santo Spirito, Passopisciaro, 95012 Castiglione di Sicilia (CT), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Tornatore ★ 4.4

Etna DOCNerello Mascalese, Nerello Cappuccio, Carricante, CatarrattoFounded 1865Etna DOC

The Tornatore family has farmed on Etna since 1865, with a large single-vineyard block in the Castiglione di Sicilia hills. Their estate is often cited as Etna DOC's largest single property, producing a full range of DOC wines from Nerello and Carricante.

Tasting: Guided tours and tastings at the estate, described as the largest single vineyard in Etna DOC. Contact +39 337 1087964.

Signature wines: tornatore-etna-bianco, tornatore-etna-rosso, tornatore-trimarchisa

Via Pietramarina 8, 95012 Castiglione di Sicilia (CT), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Tenute Nicosia ★ 4.4

Etna DOC, Cerasuolo di Vittoria DOCG, Sicilia DOCNerello Mascalese, Nerello Cappuccio, Carricante, Nero d'Avola, Frappato, CatarrattoORGANIC CERTIFIEDEtna DOC

Tenute Nicosia operates vineyards on Etna and in Vittoria, producing organic wines across a broad range including Etna DOC and Cerasuolo di Vittoria DOCG. Their Trecastagni winery on the south-east slope of Etna is a key wine-tourism destination.

Tasting: Guided tours and tastings by reservation at the Trecastagni winery. Multiple tasting packages including Contrade dell'Etna. Contact info@cantinenicosia.it.

Signature wines: nicosia-etna-rosso-fondo-filara, nicosia-etna-bianco, nicosia-monte-gorna

Via Luigi Capuana 65, 95039 Trecastagni (CT), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Baglio del Cristo di Campobello ★ 4.4

Sicilia DOCNero d'Avola, Syrah, Cabernet Sauvignon, Nerello Mascalese, Grillo, Inzolia, CatarrattoFounded 2000ORGANIC CERTIFIEDSicilia DOC

Founded in 2000 by the Bonetta family near Agrigento, Baglio del Cristo di Campobello farms 50 hectares on calcareous chalky soils 8 kilometres from the sea. Their Adennzia white and Lu Patri red have earned international recognition for quality Sicilian DOC wines.

Tasting: Winery visits and tastings at the Campobello di Licata estate. Contact via the estate website.

Signature wines: baglio-cristo-adenzia, baglio-cristo-lu-patri

Contrada Favarotta SS123 Km 19+200, 92023 Campobello di Licata (AG), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Settesoli Mandrarossa ★ 4.2

Menfi DOC, Sicilia DOCNero d'Avola, Syrah, Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, Grillo, Catarratto, Inzolia, Chardonnay, Grecanico, FianoFounded 1958Menfi DOC

Cantine Settesoli is the cooperative giant of Menfi DOC, founded in 1958, with Mandrarossa as its premium terroir-selection brand launched in 1999. Over 2,000 grower-members supply grapes from more than 6,000 hectares, making this the largest wine operation in Sicily.

Tasting: Tuesday to Saturday 09:30 to 18:00. Guided cellar visits and wine tastings. Reservations required.

Signature wines: mandrarossa-terre-di-giumara, mandrarossa-timperosse, mandrarossa-nero-davola

Contrada Puccia, 92013 Menfi (AG), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Tenuta Gatti ★ 4.2

Sicilia DOCNero d'Avola, Nerello Mascalese, Nocera, Catarratto, Inzolia, Grillo, Pinot Nero, Cabernet Sauvignon, MerlotFounded 1825ORGANIC CERTIFIEDFaro DOC

A historic organic estate spanning 217 hectares founded in 1825 at Librizzi in the Messina province, set on the Nebrodi slopes with views to the Aeolian Islands. Tenuta Gatti cultivates an unusually diverse mix of native and international varieties.

Tasting: Visits and tastings at the historic Nebrodi estate with views toward the Aeolian Islands. Contact via winetourism.com.

Signature wines: tenuta-gatti-pinot-nero, tenuta-gatti-nero-davola

Via Vignola, 98064 Librizzi (ME), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Cantina Rallo ★ 4.1

Marsala DOC, Alcamo DOC, Pantelleria DOCGrillo, Catarratto, Inzolia, Nero d'Avola, ZibibboFounded 1860Marsala DOC

Founded in 1860 by Diego Rallo, one of Marsala's oldest historic families. The Vesco family acquired the estate in 1996, integrating vineyards in Alcamo and Pantelleria with the historic Marsala cellars. Today the estate operates across western Sicily's key appellations.

Tasting: Historic Marsala cellar visits and tastings. Contact via the Rallo website.

Signature wines: rallo-marsala-superiore, rallo-alcamo-bianco

Via Vincenzo Florio 2, 91025 Marsala (TP), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Cantine Milazzo ★ 4.1

Mamertino DOC, Sicilia DOCNero d'Avola, Nocera, Nerello Mascalese, Grillo, CatarrattoMamertino DOC

Cantine Milazzo is one of the few producers working seriously within the rare Mamertino DOC, an appellation near Milazzo in the Messina province with roots in ancient Roman viniculture. Their Nocera-blended reds reflect this underexplored north-east terroir.

Tasting: Winery visits and tastings at the Milazzo estate near the Tyrrhenian coast. Contact via the winery.

Signature wines: milazzo-mamertino-rosso, milazzo-nero-davola

Contrada Feudo Soprano, 98057 Milazzo (ME), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Duca di Salaparuta (Corvo) ★ 4.3

Sicilia DOCNero d'Avola, Catarratto, Grillo, Inzolia, PerriconeFounded 1824Sicilia DOC

Founded in 1824 by the Duke of Salaparuta, Duca di Salaparuta (Corvo) is one of Sicily's oldest commercial wine estates. Their Duca Enrico is a landmark 100% Nero d'Avola and the Bianca di Valguarnera a prestigious Inzolia white. Now owned by Illva Saronno.

Tasting: Visits and tastings at the historic Casteldaccia winery. Contact via the Duca di Salaparuta website.

Signature wines: duca-di-salaparuta-bianca-di-valguarnera, duca-enrico

Via Nazionale SS 113, 90040 Casteldaccia (PA), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Tenuta Rapitala ★ 4.3

Sicilia DOCNero d'Avola, Syrah, Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, Catarratto, Chardonnay, GrilloFounded 1968Sicilia DOC

Tenuta Rapitala at Camporeale in the Palermo province was established by a Franco-Sicilian partnership in 1968. The estate produces a broad range of Sicilian varietal and blended wines across 170 hectares, with the Grand Cru their prestigious top bottling.

Tasting: Visits and tastings at the Camporeale estate. Contact via the Rapitala website.

Signature wines: rapitala-grand-cru, rapitala-hugonis

Contrada Rapitala, 90043 Camporeale (PA), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Abele Ajello ★ 4.1

Alcamo DOC, Sicilia DOCNero d'Avola, Catarratto, Grillo, InzoliaORGANIC CERTIFIEDAlcamo DOC

Abele Ajello is a small organic producer in the Belice Valley near Castelvetrano, working with Catarratto and Nero d'Avola in the western Sicilian interior.

Tasting: Visits by appointment in the Belice Valley near Castelvetrano. Contact via the winery.

Signature wines: ajello-osa, ajello-tajuni-catarratto

Via dei Mille 33, 91022 Castelvetrano (TP), Sicily, Italy → Book →

Cantine Florio ★ 4.6

Grillo, Catarratto, InzoliaAppointment onlyMarsala

Historic 1833 tufa-stone cellars on the Marsala seafront housing 3,000-plus oak barrels; guided tours cover Marsala history and production secrets.

Tip: Book at least a week ahead in summer; tours run in Italian, English and French.

Via Vincenzo Florio 1, 91025 Marsala (TP), Sicily →

Cantine Pellegrino ★ 4.5

Grillo, Catarratto, ZibibboAppointment onlyMarsala

Seven-generation family winery since 1880 with over 1,000 vats and barrels in the historic cellars; tour includes Punic-era archaeological finds, a Sicilian cart museum and a master cooper workshop.

Tip: Ask for the Ouverture tasting room with panoramic views of the Egadi Islands.

Via Paolo Pellegrino 39, 91025 Marsala (TP), Sicily →

Valdibella ★ 4.5

Sicilia DOC / Terre Siciliane IGTCatarratto, Nero d'Avola, Perricone, GrilloFounded 1998ORGANIC CERTIFIEDCamporeale, Palermo province

Small organic cooperative in Camporeale working with small-plot farmers on native Sicilian varieties. Among the most authentic affordable organics on the island.

Tip: Look for Valdibella wines at natural wine shops in Palermo and Trapani before they disappear into export channels. The Catarratto is one of the most overlooked native whites on the island.

Via Roma 9, 90044 Camporeale, Palermo, Italy →

Tasca d'Almerita Regaleali Bianco Sicilia DOC ★ 4.3

Sicilia DOCCatarratto, Inzolia, Grecanico, GrilloEUR 12-15 retail

The flagship entry-level white from the Regaleali estate in the Sicilian interior. A blend of native white varieties with fresh citrus, white almond and floral character. Drink within two years of vintage for its freshest expression.

Tip: Regaleali Bianco is one of the most reliably fresh and well-made Sicilian whites at entry-level pricing. Look for it at any decent Italian enoteca under EUR 14.

Duca di Salaparuta Corvo Bianco Sicilia DOC ★ 4.0

Sicilia DOCCatarratto, InzoliaEUR 8-12 retail

Corvo Bianco is one of Sicily's most widely distributed whites, a fresh, clean Catarratto-Inzolia blend from Duca di Salaparuta. Light citrus, melon and crisp acidity. Entry-level and widely available; benchmark approachable Sicilian white at everyday pricing.

Tip: Found at virtually every Italian supermarket and exported widely; the most reliably available Sicilian white under EUR 10 internationally.

Benanti Etna Bianco DOC ★ 4.5

Etna Bianco DOCCarricante, CatarrattoEUR 18-22 retail

Benanti's estate-level Etna Bianco is the benchmark for the Carricante variety at entry price. Volcanic minerality, green citrus, fennel and a saline finish. Made from pre-phylloxera alberello vines on the eastern Etna slopes, this is the most accessible and affordable Benanti white.

Tip: At EUR 18-22, the Benanti Etna Bianco is the most price-efficient way to taste the volcanic character of Carricante on its own appellation, without paying premium-contrada pricing.
