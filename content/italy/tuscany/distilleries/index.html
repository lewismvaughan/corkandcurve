Distilleries in Tuscany

Distilleria Nannoni ★ 4.7

Grappa di ToscanaMaremma

Founded 1973 in the Maremma by Gioacchino Nannoni, now run by Priscilla Occhipinti. The most awarded Italian grappa producer with 150+ international medals; specialises in cru grappa from single Tuscan varietals.

Tasting: Distillery visits and grappa flights by appointment; gin and brandy masterclasses also available

Localita Aratrice 8, 58045 Civitella Paganico, Italy → Book →

Distilleria Deta ★ 4.4

Grappa di Toscana, Acquavite di VinoChianti Classico

Founded 1926 by the Torrigiani di Santa Cristina family in Chianti Classico. Tuscan grappa specialist combining traditional techniques with modern eco-distilling; multiple London Spirits silver medals.

Tasting: Distillery visits and tastings by appointment; full Tuscan grappa flight from EUR 15

Strada di Poneta 2/16, 50028 Barberino Tavarnelle, Italy → Book →

Distillerie Bonollo Torrita di Siena ★ 4.4

Grappa di Toscana, Acquavite di VinoVino Nobile di Montepulciano

Bonollo's Tuscan flagship plant in Torrita di Siena, on the historic site of an ancient distillery rebuilt in 1976. Continuous and discontinuous alembics; pomace sourced from Brunello, Nobile and Chianti Classico estates.

Tasting: Distillery visits and tastings by appointment for groups; educational tours available

Strada Provinciale 326 di Sinalunga, 53049 Torrita di Siena, Italy → Book →

Liquorificio Morelli ★ 4.3

Grappa di Toscana, LiquoriValdera

Founded 1911 by Leonello Morelli in the Valdera, this family-run liquorificio is among Tuscany's oldest, producing grappa from continuous distillation of certified Tuscan pomace plus traditional liqueurs.

Tasting: Distillery and shop visits by appointment; structured tasting flights of grappa and liqueurs

Via Meucci 2, Localita Montanelli, 56036 Palaia, Italy → Book →

Distilleria Toscana Colle Val d'Elsa ★ 4.2

Grappa di Toscana, GinVal d'Elsa

Colle Val d'Elsa distillery founded from a family tradition of distillation spanning over 50 years. Produces single-variety Tuscan grappa plus a botanical gin with juniper from the Maremma macchia.

Tasting: Distillery tours and tastings by appointment; gin distillation masterclasses on request

Via Cervaione 33, 53034 Colle di Val d'Elsa, Italy → Book →

Distilleries in Tuscany, FAQ

When is the best time to visit Tuscany for wine?

Peak wine-travel season in Tuscany is spring through autumn, with harvest the standout window.

Do I need an appointment to taste at Tuscany estates?

classified-growth and grand-cru estates require booking days to weeks ahead; smaller family domaines often take walk-ins midweek.

What hours do Tuscany cellars and tasting rooms keep?

most estates open 10:00 to 17:00 by appointment, often closed Sunday and Monday.

How does tipping work at Tuscany tastings?

tipping is not expected at tastings; buying a bottle from the cellar door is the customary thank-you.

What is the one wine to try in Tuscany?

Ask the next local you meet what they would order. Tuscany rewards trust.
