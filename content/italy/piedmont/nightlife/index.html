Wine Bars Late in Piedmont

Enoteca Botz ★ 4.4

12 by the glassVanchiglia (Turin)

Vanchiglia natural-wine bar and shop pouring small-grower Italian and Central European bottles late into the night, with local seasonal snacks.

The list: Natural and low-intervention wines from Italy, Slovenia, France and Spain, with a rotating by-the-glass list strong on macerated whites and chilled sparkling

Tip: Open to 3am on Friday and Saturday; ask for a chilled macerated white and a plate of seasonal crostini.

Via Santa Giulia 48/A, 10124 Torino, Italy →

Vincafe ★ 4.3

30 by the glassAlba

Rustic-elegant wine bar, cafe and restaurant on Alba's main street, strong on Barolo and Barbaresco by the glass through the evening.

The list: Deep Langhe and Roero list led by Barolo and Barbaresco, with many older vintages by the glass alongside a full kitchen

Tip: On Alba's central street; perfect for a late Barolo flight after the truffle market closes.

Via Vittorio Emanuele II 12, 12051 Alba, Italy →

Ape Wine Bar ★ 4.2

20 by the glassAlba

Wine bar on Alba's Piazza Risorgimento owned by local producers, pouring Barbaresco and Barolo until late with small plates of regional fare.

The list: Producer-owned list heavy on local Barbaresco and Barolo, served with tapas-style small plates of Langhe ingredients

Tip: On Alba's cathedral square; the producer ownership shows in a sharp, value-led by-the-glass list.

Piazza Risorgimento 3, 12051 Alba, Italy →

BER Enoteca ★ 4.1

10 by the glassCit Turin (Turin)

Cit Turin natural-wine enoteca with a tightly chosen Piedmontese and Italian list poured by the glass into the evening.

The list: Small natural and artisanal list with a strong Piedmontese core, by the glass and bottle with a short food menu

Tip: A neighbourhood natural-wine bar away from the centre; go for the evening service after 6:15pm.

Via Giambattista Gropello 21, 10138 Torino, Italy →

Caffe dell'Orologio ★ 4.2

14 by the glassSan Salvario (Turin)

Modern San Salvario wine bar, osteria and shop pouring natural and traditional Piedmontese wines with a seasonal kitchen into the evening.

The list: Natural and traditional wines from Piedmont and beyond, by the glass and bottle, with a small osteria kitchen of seasonal local dishes

Tip: A neighbourhood San Salvario staple; let the staff steer you through the natural pours alongside the daily kitchen.

Via Oddino Morgari 16/A, 10125 Torino, Italy →

Enoteca Mastrovini ★ 4.0

12 by the glassSan Salvario (Turin)

San Salvario enoteca by Porta Nuova pouring Piedmontese and Italian wines by the glass, open to 2am Thursday to Saturday.

The list: Italian and Piedmontese list by the glass and bottle at the edge of San Salvario by Porta Nuova, open well past midnight on weekends

Tip: Steps from Porta Nuova station; weekend hours run to 2am, a reliable late stop near the trains.

Via Nizza 29, 10125 Torino, Italy →

Banco Vini e Alimenti ★ 4.1

12 by the glassQuadrilatero Romano (Turin)

Charming Quadrilatero Romano natural-wine bar and grocer pouring interesting bottles from Piedmont and beyond with seasonal small plates.

The list: Globe-spanning natural-wine list with a Piedmontese backbone, paired with a small seasonal kitchen of local produce

Tip: Low-key Quadrilatero spot; let the staff guide you through the by-the-glass natural pours.

Via Giovanni Botero 11F, 10122 Torino, Italy →

Listening Bars in Piedmont

Enoteca Vinile ★ 4.3

10 by the glassCentro (Turin)

Cosy central-Turin enoteca where natural wines are poured to a soundtrack of the owner's vinyl collection, with crostini to match.

The list: Natural and traditional wines by the glass chosen by a music-loving owner, paired with crostini and old vinyl on the turntable

Tip: Tell the owner what you are listening to; the wine recommendation usually follows the record.

Via Principe Amedeo 21/L, 10123 Torino, Italy →

Vinile Live Restaurant ★ 4.0

15 by the glassCollina (Turin)

Hillside Turin venue pairing wine and cocktails with live music and DJ sets, a roomier listening-bar option above the city.

The list: Wine and cocktail list built around live music and DJ nights on the Turin hillside, with a full evening kitchen

Tip: Up on the Turin hill; check the gig calendar and book if there is a live set that night.

Strada al Traforo di Pino 106, 10132 Torino, Italy →

Smile Tree ★ 4.1

8 by the glassQuadrilatero Romano (Turin)

Tiny, design-conscious Quadrilatero bar known for experimental cocktails, vermouth and a curated soundtrack on Piazza della Consolata.

The list: Research-driven cocktails and a short wine and vermouth list in a tiny design-led room with curated music

Tip: Seats are scarce in this micro-bar; arrive early and let the bartender steer between cocktails and vermouth pours.

Piazza della Consolata 9, 10122 Torino, Italy →

Candle Lit in Piedmont

Caffe Al Bicerin ★ 4.5

8 by the glassQuadrilatero Romano (Turin)

Candlelit 1763 cafe facing the Consolata sanctuary, with marble tables, mirrored panelling and a vermouth and wine pour list.

The list: Historic 1763 cafe pouring vermouth, rosolio and Piedmontese wines beneath mirrored wood panelling and lamplight

Tip: The bicerin (coffee, chocolate, cream) is the house ritual; pair an early-evening vermouth before the cafe closes.

Piazza della Consolata 5, 10122 Torino, Italy →

Vinoteca Centro Storico ★ 4.6

15 by the glassBarolo (Serralunga d'Alba)

Intimate, low-lit wine bar below Serralunga castle run by a husband-and-wife team, with prosciutto, a handwritten menu and deep Barolo.

The list: Handwritten list of Barolo, Barbaresco, Champagne and Burgundy in a tiny low-lit room beneath the Serralunga castle

Tip: Friday nights are busiest; the candlelit cellar room under the castle is the most romantic table in the Barolo zone.

Via Cappellano 1, 12050 Serralunga d'Alba, Italy →

Caffe Mulassano ★ 4.4

6 by the glassCentro (Turin)

Jewel-box 1907 cafe on Piazza Castello, all carved wood and gilt, serving its own house vermouth bianco at the marble counter.

The list: Belle-epoque cafe serving its own house vermouth bianco and rosolio delle Alpi at a marble counter under carved ceilings

Tip: Order the house Liquore delle Alpi vermouth from the 1879 recipe; the tiny gilded room is best at dusk.

Piazza Castello 15, 10123 Torino, Italy →

Lounges in Piedmont

La Drogheria ★ 4.2

18 by the glassPiazza Vittorio (Turin)

Iconic late-night lounge bar on Piazza Vittorio Veneto, mixing Turin vermouth, spirits and wine from aperitivo hour into the small hours.

The list: Sprawling spirits and Turin vermouth selection alongside wines by the glass, a late-night lounge over the great square

Tip: Grab a sofa overlooking Piazza Vittorio Veneto; it runs from early aperitivo straight through to late-night drinks.

Piazza Vittorio Veneto 18/D, 10123 Torino, Italy →

Affini ★ 4.3

14 by the glassSan Salvario (Turin)

San Salvario mixology lounge famous for an enormous vermouth and amaro range plus a sharp wine list and creative tapas.

The list: One of Turin's widest vermouth and amaro selections alongside a curated wine list, mixed into cocktails or poured neat

Tip: The vermouth wall is the draw; ask the bartender for a Vermouth di Torino flight before dinner tapas.

Via Belfiore 16/C, 10125 Torino, Italy →

Baratti & Milano ★ 4.3

8 by the glassGalleria Subalpina (Turin)

Opulent 1875 cafe in the Galleria Subalpina, founded by vermouth and liqueur merchants, serving an aperitivo amid mirrors and chandeliers.

The list: Historic 1875 confectioner and cafe whose founders traded vermouth, syrups and liqueurs, still serving an aperitivo under chandeliers

Tip: Take an early-evening vermouth in the Galleria Subalpina's gilded salon, a Savoy-era lounge in all but name.

Piazza Castello 27, 10123 Torino, Italy →

Fortified Specialists in Piedmont

Casa Martini ★ 4.3

6 by the glassPessione (Chieri)

The Martini & Rossi headquarters near Turin, with a vermouth museum and guided tastings of the full Vermouth di Torino range.

The list: Home of Martini & Rossi vermouth with guided tastings of the full Vermouth di Torino range and a museum of the aperitivo

Tip: Book the vermouth class to blend your own bottle; reachable in 20 minutes by train from Turin Porta Susa.

Piazza Luigi Rossi 2, frazione Pessione, 10023 Chieri, Italy →

Caffe Mulassano vermouth counter ★ 4.4

5 by the glassCentro (Turin)

The historic Piazza Castello cafe whose 1879 house vermouth bianco and rosolio make it a living shrine to Turin's fortified-wine roots.

The list: House Vermouth di Torino bianco and Rosolio delle Alpi made to an 1879 recipe, poured at the historic marble bar

Tip: This is where Italian aperitivo culture took shape; drink the house vermouth bianco standing at the counter.

Piazza Castello 15, 10123 Torino, Italy →

Affini vermouth wall ★ 4.2

14 by the glassSan Salvario (Turin)

San Salvario bar whose vermouth-and-amaro wall is the best place in Turin to taste a flight of Vermouth di Torino styles late.

The list: Dedicated wall of Turin and Italian vermouths and amari, served neat, on the rocks or built into bespoke cocktails

Tip: Order a flight straight from the vermouth wall to taste several Vermouth di Torino styles side by side.

Via Belfiore 16/C, 10125 Torino, Italy →

Late Tastings in Piedmont

All'Enoteca ★ 4.4

20 by the glassRoero (Canale)

Canale fine-dining address with a serious Roero and Langhe cellar, offering evening wine flights alongside its tasting menu.

The list: Michelin-level Roero and Langhe wine programme tied to a tasting-menu kitchen, with evening flights of Roero Arneis and Nebbiolo

Tip: Book the evening service in Canale and let the sommelier pair Roero Arneis and Nebbiolo across the tasting menu.

Via Roma 57, 12043 Canale, Italy →

Rossorubino ★ 4.1

20 by the glassSan Salvario (Turin)

Spacious San Salvario wine shop and enoteca near Porta Nuova, with by-the-glass tastings and evening flights across Piedmont and Italy.

The list: Large enoteca near Porta Nuova with by-the-glass tastings spanning Piedmont and Italy, plus regular evening tasting flights

Tip: A few steps from Porta Nuova; ask about the evening tasting flights to compare Langhe and Monferrato wines.

Via Madama Cristina 21, 10125 Torino, Italy →

Enoteca Fracchia & Berchialla ★ 4.2

25 by the glassAlba

Long-established Alba wine shop and tasting bar in the historic centre, pouring cru Barolo and Barbaresco by the glass into the evening.

The list: Historic central-Alba wine shop and bar with a vast Langhe range, offering by-the-glass tastings of Barolo and Barbaresco crus

Tip: Ask to taste two Barolo crus side by side; the shop's range spans most of the Barolo MGA map.

Via Vittorio Emanuele II 17, 12051 Alba, Italy →

Vincafe evening Barolo flight ★ 4.2

30 by the glassAlba

Vincafe's evening Barolo and Barbaresco by-the-glass flights make it Alba's easiest late-night Nebbiolo tasting on the main street.

The list: Curated evening flights of Barolo and Barbaresco by the glass, including mature vintages, on Alba's central street

Tip: Build a vertical of one Barolo cru across vintages; the by-the-glass program runs late into the evening.

Via Vittorio Emanuele II 12, 12051 Alba, Italy →

Coppo's UNESCO-listed Canelli cellars, the underground cathedrals, where the house ages its Metodo Classico sparkling for guided tastings.

Nightlife in Piedmont, FAQ

When is the best time to visit Piedmont for wine?

Peak wine-travel season in Piedmont is spring through autumn, with harvest the standout window.

Do I need an appointment to taste at Piedmont estates?

classified-growth and grand-cru estates require booking days to weeks ahead; smaller family domaines often take walk-ins midweek.

What hours do Piedmont cellars and tasting rooms keep?

most estates open 10:00 to 17:00 by appointment, often closed Sunday and Monday.

How does tipping work at Piedmont tastings?

tipping is not expected at tastings; buying a bottle from the cellar door is the customary thank-you.

What is the one wine to try in Piedmont?

Ask the next local you meet what they would order. Piedmont rewards trust.
