Wachau Wine Guide

Danube terraces, gneiss soils, the Smaragd standard

Updated May 2026 By Cork & Curve Editorial · Austria

A UNESCO World Heritage valley carved by the Danube between Melk and Krems, where gneiss and loess terraces produce dry Grüner Veltliner and Riesling under the Steinfeder-Federspiel-Smaragd tier system.

Signature wines of Wachau

The iconic bottles that define Wachau.

F.X. Pichler Riesling Smaragd Kellerberg

Producer: fx-pichler

Single-vineyard Riesling Smaragd from the Kellerberg Ried above Oberloiben. Slate mineral, lime zest and dried apricot with long ageing potential.

Price: EUR 70-110 at retail

F.X. Pichler Grüner Veltliner Smaragd Loibenberg

Producer: fx-pichler

Full-bodied Smaragd Grüner Veltliner from the Loibenberg Ried: ripe stone fruit and white-pepper spice on a broad mineral backbone with persistent finish.

Price: EUR 60-90 at retail

Emmerich Knoll Riesling Smaragd Loibenberg

Producer: emmerich-knoll

Benchmark Smaragd Riesling from the Loibenberg Ried: apricot, chalk mineral and bright acidity. Fuder-aged; rewarding 8-12 years of bottle age.

Price: EUR 50-75 at retail

Emmerich Knoll Riesling Smaragd Schütt

Producer: emmerich-knoll

Structured Smaragd Riesling from the Schütt Ried above Loiben: Fuder-aged with stone-fruit depth, chalk mineral and firm, persistent acidity.

Price: EUR 55-80 at retail

Prager Riesling Smaragd Achleiten

Producer: prager

Prager Achleiten Riesling Smaragd from the celebrated amphibolite-gneiss Ried: apricot, crushed granite and exotic spice with firm, structured acidity.

Price: EUR 55-80 at retail

Prager Grüner Veltliner Smaragd Wachstum Bodenstein

Producer: prager

Reserve Grüner Veltliner Smaragd from Prager's select Achleiten parcels: intense pepper spice, concentrated stone fruit and exceptional mineral length.

Price: EUR 70-120 at retail

Vineyards to know in Wachau

A handful of the estates we send friends to when they are in Wachau.

Weingut F.X. Pichler

Wachau DACGrüner Veltliner, Riesling, Gelber MuskatellerOberloiben 27, 3601 Dürnstein, Austria

Seven-generation Pichler estate in Oberloiben producing Smaragd Grüner Veltliner and Riesling from the Kellerberg and Loibenberg sites. 20 hectares planted half Grüner Veltliner, half Riesling.

Signature: fx-pichler-kellerberg-riesling-smaragd, fx-pichler-loibenberg-gruner-smaragd, fx-pichler-unendlich

More about Weingut F.X. Pichler →

Weingut Emmerich Knoll

Wachau DACGrüner Veltliner, Riesling, Gelber MuskatellerUnterloiben 132, 3601 Dürnstein, Austria

Family estate since 1825 at Unterloiben, working Schütt, Loibenberg, Pfaffenberg and Kreutles sites. Celebrated for age-worthy Riesling and Grüner Veltliner Smaragd bottlings.

Signature: knoll-schütt-riesling-smaragd, knoll-loibenberg-gruner-smaragd, knoll-pfaffenberg-riesling-smaragd

More about Weingut Emmerich Knoll →

Weingut Prager

Wachau DACRiesling, Grüner VeltlinerWachaustrasse 48, 3610 Weissenkirchen in der Wachau, Austria

Bodenstein-run estate in Weissenkirchen with 18 hectares split across Achleiten, Klaus, Steinriegl, Hinter der Burg and Hollerin sites. The Wachstum Bodenstein series bottles selected Achleiten parcels as Smaragd.

Signature: prager-wachstum-bodenstein-riesling-smaragd, prager-wachstum-bodenstein-gruner-smaragd, prager-achleiten-riesling-smaragd

More about Weingut Prager →

Weingut Franz Hirtzberger

Wachau DACRiesling, Grüner VeltlinerKremserstraße 8, 3620 Spitz an der Donau, Austria

Five-generation estate in Spitz known for the Singerriedel Riesling from gneiss-granite terraces above Spitz. Honivogl Grüner Veltliner Smaragd is a warm-year, full-bodied expression of Wachau Veltliner.

Signature: hirtzberger-singerriedel-riesling-smaragd, hirtzberger-honivogl-gruner-smaragd, hirtzberger-hochrain-riesling-smaragd

More about Weingut Franz Hirtzberger →

Nikolaihof Wachau

Wachau DACGrüner Veltliner, Riesling, NeuburgerNikolaigasse 3, 3512 Mautern an der Donau, Austria

Oldest documented winery in Austria (nearly 2,000 years of history) at Mautern on the south bank. Demeter biodynamic since 1971. Famous for the Vom Stein Riesling Smaragd and long-aged Hefeabzug Grüner Veltliner.

Signature: nikolaihof-vom-stein-riesling-smaragd, nikolaihof-hefeabzug-gruner-smaragd, nikolaihof-vinothek-riesling

More about Nikolaihof Wachau →

Domäne Wachau

Wachau DACGrüner Veltliner, RieslingDürnstein 107, 3601 Dürnstein, Austria

Wachau's largest estate, representing around 250 vintner families across 440 hectares. Founded 1938 as Winzergenossenschaft Wachau, renamed Domäne Wachau in 2008. Roman Horvath MW oversees the range alongside Heinz Frischengruber.

Signature: domaene-wachau-achleiten-gruner-smaragd, domaene-wachau-kellerberg-riesling-smaragd, domaene-wachau-terrasen-federspiel-gruner

Wachau wine, FAQ

When is the best time to visit Wachau for wine?

Peak wine-travel season in Wachau is spring through autumn, with harvest the standout window.

Do I need an appointment to taste at Wachau estates?

classified-growth and grand-cru estates require booking days to weeks ahead; smaller family domaines often take walk-ins midweek.

What hours do Wachau cellars and tasting rooms keep?

most estates open 10:00 to 17:00 by appointment, often closed Sunday and Monday.

How does tipping work at Wachau tastings?

tipping is not expected at tastings; buying a bottle from the cellar door is the customary thank-you.

What is the one wine to try in Wachau?

If you only open one bottle, open F.X. Pichler Riesling Smaragd Kellerberg by fx-pichler. It is the wine most associated with Wachau.
