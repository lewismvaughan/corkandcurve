Weingut Josef Jamek ★ 4.5

Joching estate founded in 1912. Josef Jamek was a pioneer of dry, varietal Wachau wines in the 1960s. The estate restaurant remains open on the Danube. Julia Jamek and Herwig Maleta run it today.

Vineyard in Wachau · Joching· Wachau DAC· 25 ha Verified May 28, 2026

Weingut Hofstätter ★ 4.3

Wachau DACGrüner Veltliner, Riesling, Neuburger, Gelber Muskateller, Weissburgunder9 haFounded 1930Petra and Wolfgang HofstätterWinemaker Wolfgang HofstätterSpitz

Third-generation family estate at Spitz foot working 8.5 hectares from St. Michael to Schwallenbach. Singerriedel, Hartberg and Tausendeimerberg are key sites. Gelber Muskateller and Frühroter Veltliner add variety to the portfolio.

Tasting: Seasonal Heurigen (wine tavern) on site; direct sales available.

Signature wines: hofstaetter-singerriedel-riesling-smaragd, hofstaetter-tausendeimerberg-gruner-smaragd

Tip: The Gelber Muskateller Federspiel is one of the few compelling Muscats in the Wachau.

Mathias Hirtzberger set up his own estate in Wösendorf in 2014, independent from the Franz Hirtzberger domaine. The Hofmeisterei restaurant attached to the winery is a wine-tourism draw.

Weingut Schrey ★ 4.2

Wachau DACGrüner Veltliner, Riesling6 haFounded 1920Winemaker Rudi SchreyWösendorf

Family estate in Wösendorf working steep sites at Höll, Kirchweg, Kollmütz and Hochrain. A Vinea Wachau member committed to site-specific farming with active green cover management.

Tasting: Direct cellar-door sales; vineyard walk tastings by arrangement.

Signature wines: schrey-hochrain-riesling-smaragd, schrey-holl-gruner-federspiel

Tip: Vineyard walks with tasting can be arranged for groups wanting an off-the-beaten-path Wachau experience.
