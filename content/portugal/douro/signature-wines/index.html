The iconic bottles that define Douro Valley: what they are, who makes them and what they cost.

Iconic bottles of Douro Valley

Barca Velha ★ 5.0

ferreirastill red DOC DouroTouriga Nacional, Touriga Franca, Tinta Roriz, Tinta Barroca10 to 40 from vintageEUR 400 to 700 at retail

Barca Velha, Portugal's most iconic dry red, made by Ferreira only in exceptional Douro Superior vintages. DOC Douro, from the Douro Valley of northern Portugal.

Tasting notes: Portugal's most iconic dry red, made only in exceptional years from Douro Superior fruit: a structured, ageworthy wine with cedar, tobacco and a long mineral finish. Source: producer and Wine Advocate 97 points 2011 vintage.

Quinta do Noval Nacional ★ 5.0

quinta-do-novalfortified PortTouriga Nacional, Touriga Franca, Tinta Roriz, Sousao30 to 80 from vintageEUR 1500 to 6000 at release

Quinta do Noval Nacional, from a tiny block of ungrafted pre-phylloxera vines, one of the rarest and most legendary Ports in the world.

Tasting notes: One of the rarest and most legendary wines in the world, from a tiny block of ungrafted, pre-phylloxera vines, declared only in exceptional years and capable of ageing for generations. Source: producer and Wine Advocate 100 points 2011 vintage.

Quinta do Vale Meao Tinto ★ 4.8

quinta-do-vale-meaostill red DOC DouroTouriga Nacional, Touriga Franca, Tinta Roriz, Tinta Barroca, Tinto Cao8 to 30 from vintageEUR 90 to 160 at retail

Quinta do Vale Meao Tinto, one of Portugal's most sought-after dry reds, from the historic estate where Barca Velha was born. Best enjoyed 8 to 30 from vintage.

Tasting notes: One of Portugal's most sought-after dry reds, from the historic Douro Superior estate where Barca Velha was born: powerful yet refined, with cedar, graphite and a long structured finish. Source: producer and Wine Advocate 96 points 2017 vintage.

Taylor's Vintage Port ★ 4.9

taylorsVintage PortTouriga Nacional, Touriga Franca, Tinta Roriz, Tinta Barroca, Tinto Cao20 to 50 from vintageEUR 80 to 160 at release

Taylor's declared Vintage Port, the firm and long-lived classic benchmark from the Fladgate house, built around the Vargellas estate.

Tasting notes: Taylor's declared Vintage is the firm, structured benchmark of the style: dense black-fruit concentration over grippy tannins built for decades in bottle. Source: producer and Wine Advocate 98 points 2016 vintage.

Dow's Vintage Port ★ 4.8

dowsVintage PortTouriga Nacional, Touriga Franca, Tinta Roriz, Tinta Barroca, Sousao20 to 50 from vintageEUR 90 to 170 at release

Dow's Vintage Port, the driest of the classic Vintages, built around Quinta do Bomfim with a firm savoury finish. Best enjoyed 20 to 50 from vintage.

Tasting notes: The driest of the classic Vintage Ports, built around Quinta do Bomfim and Senhora da Ribeira fruit, with a firm structure and a notably dry, savoury finish. Source: producer and Wine Spectator 98 points 2016 vintage.

Graham's Vintage Port ★ 4.8

grahamsVintage PortTouriga Nacional, Touriga Franca, Tinta Barroca, Tinta Roriz, Tinto Cao20 to 50 from vintageEUR 90 to 170 at release

Graham's Vintage Port, the richest and sweetest of the classic Vintages, built on warm Malvedos fruit. DOC Porto, from the Douro Valley of northern Portugal.

Tasting notes: The richest and sweetest of the classic Vintage Ports, built on the warm Malvedos and Tua fruit, with opulent black fruit over a firm spine. Source: producer and Wine Spectator 98 points 2017 vintage.

Fonseca Vintage Port ★ 4.8

fonsecaVintage PortTouriga Nacional, Touriga Franca, Tinta Barroca, Tinta Roriz, Tinto Cao20 to 50 from vintageEUR 80 to 150 at release

Fonseca Vintage Port, the lush exotically fruited classic of the Fladgate house, built around Quinta do Panascal. Best enjoyed 20 to 50 from vintage.

Tasting notes: Fonseca's Vintage is the lush, exotically fruited counterpoint to Taylor's: opulent black fruit and a perfumed minty lift over firm structure. Source: producer and Wine Advocate 98 points 2017 vintage.

Quinta do Vesuvio Vintage Port ★ 4.7

quinta-do-vesuvioVintage PortTouriga Nacional, Touriga Franca, Tinta Barroca, Tinta Amarela, Alicante Bouschet20 to 50 from vintageEUR 90 to 160 at release

Quinta do Vesuvio Vintage Port, a powerful single-quinta Vintage from the grand Douro Superior estate, foot-trodden in granite lagares.

Tasting notes: A powerful single-quinta Vintage Port from the grand Douro Superior estate, foot-trodden in granite lagares, with dense black fruit and a mineral, ageworthy structure. Source: producer and Wine Advocate 97 points 2017 vintage.

Niepoort Batuta ★ 4.7

niepoortstill red DOC DouroTouriga Franca, Tinta Roriz, Tinta Amarela, Touriga Nacional8 to 25 from vintageEUR 70 to 110 at retail

Niepoort Batuta, the house's flagship old-vine Douro red, concentrated yet fresh with graphite minerality. DOC Douro, from the Douro Valley of northern Portugal.

Tasting notes: Niepoort's flagship old-vine Douro red, from very old field-blend vineyards: concentrated yet fresh, with graphite minerality and fine-grained structure for long ageing. Source: producer and Wine Advocate 95 points 2017 vintage.

Quinta do Crasto Vinha Maria Teresa ★ 4.8

quinta-do-crastostill red DOC DouroTouriga Nacional, Touriga Franca, Tinta Roriz, Tinto Cao8 to 25 from vintageEUR 90 to 150 at retail

Quinta do Crasto Vinha Maria Teresa, a flagship single-vineyard old-vine field blend built for the cellar. DOC Douro, from the Douro Valley of northern Portugal.

Tasting notes: A single-vineyard old-vine field blend from a plot of pre-1920 vines, dense and structured yet fresh, with graphite minerality and a long savoury finish built for the cellar. Source: producer and Wine Advocate 96 points 2017 vintage.

Wine & Soul Pintas ★ 4.7

wine-and-soulstill red DOC DouroTouriga Nacional, Touriga Franca, Tinta Roriz, Tinta Amarela8 to 25 from vintageEUR 70 to 110 at retail

Wine & Soul Pintas, the dense mineral old-vine field blend that launched the acclaimed Vale de Mendiz project. DOC Douro, from the Douro Valley of northern Portugal.

Tasting notes: The wine that launched Wine & Soul, from a tiny plot of ancient field-blend vines at Vale de Mendiz: dense and mineral yet fresh, a benchmark of the modern Douro. Source: producer and Wine Advocate 95 points 2017 vintage.

Chryseia ★ 4.7

chryseiastill red DOC DouroTouriga Nacional, Touriga Franca6 to 20 from vintageEUR 60 to 90 at retail

Chryseia, a refined Bordeaux-influenced Douro red from the Symington and Prats partnership. DOC Douro, from the Douro Valley of northern Portugal.

Tasting notes: A refined, Bordeaux-influenced Douro red from the Symington and Prats partnership: silky and polished, prizing elegance and freshness over power. Source: producer and Wine Advocate 95 points 2017 vintage.

Signature Wines in Douro Valley, FAQ

When is the best time to visit Douro Valley for wine?

Peak wine-travel season in Douro Valley is spring through autumn, with harvest the standout window.

Do I need an appointment to taste at Douro Valley estates?

classified-growth and grand-cru estates require booking days to weeks ahead; smaller family domaines often take walk-ins midweek.

What hours do Douro Valley cellars and tasting rooms keep?

most estates open 10:00 to 17:00 by appointment, often closed Sunday and Monday.

How does tipping work at Douro Valley tastings?

tipping is not expected at tastings; buying a bottle from the cellar door is the customary thank-you.

What is the one wine to try in Douro Valley?

If you only open one bottle, open Barca Velha by ferreira. It is the wine most associated with Douro Valley.
