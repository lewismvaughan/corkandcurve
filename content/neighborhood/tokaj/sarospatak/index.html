Wine in Sárospatak, Tokaj

12 editor picks across 6 categories

Vineyards in Sárospatak

Fekete Pince ★ 3.9

Tokaj PDOFurmint, HárslevelűSárospatak

Fekete Pince in Sárospatak produces Furmint and Aszú from plots in the northern sector of the Tokaj appellation, where cooler temperatures extend the growing season and produce wines of heightened natural acidity.

Tasting: Cellar tastings in Sárospatak; visits by arrangement.

Signature wines: fekete-pince-furmint-dry, fekete-pince-aszu-5-puttonyos

Tip: Sárospatak's 16th-century Renaissance castle complex is within walking distance of the cellar; combine the tasting with a visit to the historic castle and Calvinist college.

Sárospatak, Borsod-Abaúj-Zemplén, Hungary → Book →

Winesor Pince ★ 3.9

Tokaj PDOFurmint, HárslevelűSárospatak

Winesor Pince is based in the historic town of Sárospatak, associated with Hungarian Calvinist culture and winemaking, producing dry Furmint and Aszú from the northern sector of the appellation.

Tasting: Winery visits and tastings by appointment in Sárospatak.

Signature wines: winesor-pince-furmint-dry, winesor-pince-aszu-5-puttonyos

Tip: Sárospatak combines Renaissance castle, Calvinist college and wine cellars within a walkable town centre.

Sárospatak, Borsod-Abaúj-Zemplén, Hungary → Book →

80 by the glassSárospatak

Sárospatak's leading wine bar on the main Rákóczi street, open since 2015. Friday tastings are informal guided events; among the widest Tokaji ranges available by the glass in Sárospatak.

The list: Wide selection of Tokaji wines; regular Friday-night informal guided tastings; wine and snack pairings

Rákóczi utca 21, 3950 Sárospatak, Hungary →

The list: Tokaj-Hegyalja wines by the glass; seasonal terrace; Hungarian bites

Rákóczi utca 57, 3950 Sárospatak, Hungary →

Wine Schools in Sárospatak

Tokaj-Hegyalja Egyetem Wine Business Institute ★ 4.6

Sárospatak

Hungary's dedicated wine university in Sárospatak, offering a postgraduate Wine Business Manager programme in partnership with the Burgundy School of Business in Dijon. Covers enology, wine marketing, finance, brand management. Curriculum spans global wine trade as well.

Eötvös utca 7, 3950 Sárospatak, Hungary → Book →

Wine Museums in Sárospatak

Rákóczi Castle Underground Wine Exhibition ★ 4.4

Sárospatak

Underground exhibition at the Rákóczi Castle Museum (Hungarian National Museum Rákóczi Múzeuma) dedicated to wine and viticulture of the Hegyalja. Displays old Tokaji bottles, labels, and early viticultural tools in the castle's vaulted cellars. Main building also covers Rákóczi family history.

Szent Erzsébet utca 19, 3950 Sárospatak, Hungary →

Hidden Gems in Sárospatak

Rákóczi Cellar, Sárospatak ★ 4.5

Tokaj PDOFurmint, HárslevelüSárospatak

The historic Rákóczi Cellar beneath the castle in Sárospatak is a heritage cellar, deeply connected to the 17th-century wine trade that made Tokaj the wine of European courts. Aszú and Szamorodni produced from the surrounding vineyard plots are available for tasting in the centuries-old cellar chambers.

Tip: Sárospatak's castle and cellar together provide one of the strongest single-site immersions in Tokaj heritage; combine the cellar visit with a tour of the Rákóczi Castle above it.

Sárospatak, Tokaj wine region, Hungary →

Nightlife in Sárospatak

Sárospatak riverside wine bars ★ 4.5

SárospatakUntil 23:00

Sárospatak's riverside streets below the Rákóczi Castle have a relaxed evening wine-bar culture built around local Tokaj producers. The combination of the castle backdrop, the Bodrog river terrace and locally produced Aszú and Furmint by the glass makes for an appealing late-evening setting in Tokaj.

Wine focus: Tokaji Aszú heritage pours, dry Furmint, local Szamorodni

Tip: The castle is illuminated at night; a riverside table at one of the terrace bars with a view of the Rákóczi Castle and a glass of dry Szamorodni száraz is an atmospheric wine evening in Hungary.

Kossuth Lajos utca, 3950 Sárospatak, Hungary →

Sárospatak castle courtyard evening events ★ 4.4

Sárospatak

The Rákóczi Castle courtyard in Sárospatak hosts occasional summer evening cultural and wine events, combining the historic setting of the 17th-century castle with performances and curated wine pours from the surrounding Tokaj wine region. The courtyard format is used for both music performances and harvest-related wine celebrations.

Wine focus: Sárospatak-area Tokaj wines at cultural events; Aszú and dry Furmint served

Tip: Check the castle event programme in advance; summer cultural evenings here are scheduled seasonally and are a highlight of the Sárospatak calendar.

Rákóczi Castle, Sárospatak, 3950 Hungary →

Sárospatak Rákóczi castle cellar evening ★ 4.5

Sárospatak

The Rákóczi Castle cellar at Sárospatak operates candlelit evening tastings in the 17th-century castle basement, the very site associated with the Rákóczi family's wine trading activities. Aszú and Szamorodni from the surrounding Sárospatak vineyards are poured in the historic cellar chambers during evening heritage events, particularly during festival periods.

Wine focus: Sárospatak-terroir Aszú and Szamorodni, Rákóczi heritage pours

Tip: The castle museum and the cellar operate on separate schedules; confirm opening times of the cellar evening programme with the castle administration before visiting.

Rákóczi Castle, Sárospatak, 3950 Hungary →

Sárospatak Bodrog hotel wine lounge ★ 4.2

Sárospatak

The riverside hotels in Sárospatak operate wine lounges with views across the Bodrog river to the castle, serving a curated selection of Tokaj wines and Hungarian wines by the glass. The northern Hegyalja location means local Sárospatak-terroir Furmint and Szamorodni are typically available alongside the main-region bottlings.

Wine focus: Sárospatak-adjacent Tokaj wines, Hungarian wine selection

Tip: Sárospatak is a calmer and less expensive overnight option than Tokaj town; the Bodrog riverside setting and castle views from the hotel terrace compensate for the slightly longer drive to the Mád and Tarcal cellar cores.

Kossuth Lajos utca, 3950 Sárospatak, Hungary →

Sárospatak castle cellar late tasting ★ 4.4

Sárospatak

The Sárospatak castle cellar operates extended evening tasting sessions during festival periods and summer tourism season. The cellar's historical connection to the Rákóczi family wine trade makes it a uniquely atmospheric venue for a late-evening Aszú tasting, pairing heritage context with the produced wine.

Wine focus: Rákóczi-adjacent Aszú and Szamorodni from Sárospatak-area producers

Tip: Check the castle's event calendar for scheduled late-evening cellar sessions; these are more common in July, August and during festival weekends in September and October.

Rákóczi Castle, Sárospatak, Hungary →
