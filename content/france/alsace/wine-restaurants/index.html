Cellar and plate in Alsace

L'Auberge de l'Ill ★ 4.9

Refined Alsatian gastronomy by Marc Haeberlin, with riverside dining and a deep Alsace and grand cru Burgundy cellar built over 150 years of family hostingIllhaeusern

Haeberlin family riverside inn established 1882 in Illhaeusern, two Michelin stars in 2026. Marc Haeberlin's matelote au Riesling and salmon souffle anchor a deep Alsace wine list.

Awards: Michelin 2026, Michelin 2025, Relais et Chateaux 2025

2 Rue de Collonges au Mont d'Or, 68970 Illhaeusern, France →

La Table d'Olivier Nasti ★ 4.8

Two-star gastronomic Alsatian cuisine by Meilleur Ouvrier de France Olivier Nasti, championing Vosges game, foie gras and morels with a Riesling-led cellar from sommelier Jean-Baptiste KleinKaysersberg

Two-Michelin-star gastronomic table inside Hotel Le Chambard, Kaysersberg. Olivier Nasti at the stoves and Jean-Baptiste Klein on the wine list steer a season-led tasting menu around Alsace whites and game.

Awards: Michelin 2026, Meilleur Ouvrier de France 2007, Relais et Chateaux 2025

9-13 Rue du General de Gaulle, 68240 Kaysersberg, France →

JY'S ★ 4.8

Two-star gastronomic restaurant by Jean-Yves Schillinger blending Alsace, New York and Asian influences, paired with a deep Alsace by-the-glass programmeChamp de Mars Colmar

Jean-Yves Schillinger's two-Michelin-star table in the MGallery hotel, facing Colmar's Champ de Mars park. Cuisine moves through Alsace, Brittany, Japan and the United States.

Awards: Michelin 2026

3 Allee du Champ de Mars, 68000 Colmar, France →

Buerehiesel ★ 4.6

Modern Alsace cuisine in a half-timbered 17th-century farmhouse rebuilt in the Orangerie, chef Eric Westermann pairs frog's-leg schniederspaetle with a strong Alsace cellarOrangerie park

Half-timbered 17th-century farmhouse in the Orangerie park, chef Eric Westermann earns one Michelin star with frog's-leg schniederspaetle and Alsace-led pairings.

Awards: Michelin 2026, Gault Millau 2025

4 Parc de l'Orangerie, 67000 Strasbourg, France →

Au Crocodile ★ 4.7

Historic Strasbourg gastronomic table refurbished in 2020, chef Romain Brillat (a relative of Brillat-Savarin) cooks one-Michelin-star modern French alongside a sommelier-driven cellarStrasbourg cathedral quarter

Historic Strasbourg house with the eponymous stuffed crocodile, refurbished 2020 and re-starred 2021. Chef Romain Brillat plates one-Michelin-star modern French with Alsace pairings.

Awards: Michelin 2026, Relais et Chateaux 2025

10 Rue de l'Outre, 67000 Strasbourg, France →

Le 1741 ★ 4.6

One-Michelin-star modern French cuisine across from the Palais Rohan, chef Jeremy Page and sommelier Michael Wagner build menus around Alsace, Burgundy and Champagne pairingsKrutenau

Quayside one-Michelin-star restaurant opposite the Palais Rohan, chef Jeremy Page plates precise modern French dishes paired with sommelier Michael Wagner's Alsace-led list.

Awards: Michelin 2026

22 Quai des Bateliers, 67000 Strasbourg, France →

Umami ★ 4.5

One-Michelin-star fusion of Alsatian tradition and Asian flavours by chef Rene Fieger, paired with a tight cellar of Alsace whites and biodynamic French growersPetite France

Chef Rene Fieger's one-Michelin-star table tucked in the half-timbered Petite France quarter. Market cooking that crosses Alsace and Asia, paired with biodynamic Alsace bottles.

Awards: Michelin 2026

8 Rue des Dentelles, 67000 Strasbourg, France →

Restaurant Girardin ★ 4.7

One-Michelin-star tasting-only restaurant inside La Maison des Tetes, chef Eric Girardin's 14-seat dining room and minimalist tasting menus paired with a deep Alsace cellarColmar old town

Eric Girardin's intimate 14-seat Michelin-star table inside the 1609 Maison des Tetes. Tasting menus at EUR 155 with deep Alsace pairings, set inside the Renaissance landmark.

Awards: Michelin 2026, Relais et Chateaux 2025

19 Rue des Tetes, 68000 Colmar, France →

Le Feuillage - Hostellerie La Cheneaudiere ★ 4.6

One-Michelin-star gastronomic restaurant by Jean-Paul Acker inside the five-star Cheneaudiere Relais et Chateaux, with a forest-and-vineyard tasting menu and a Riesling-deep cellarVallee de la Bruche

Hostellerie La Cheneaudiere's gastronomic restaurant in the Vosges foothills, where Jean-Paul Acker holds a Michelin star with Vosges foraging and Alsace-led pairings.

Awards: Michelin 2025, Gault Millau 2025, Relais et Chateaux 2025

3 Rue Vieux Moulin, 67420 Colroy-la-Roche, France →

Le Cerf ★ 4.5

One-Michelin-star gastronomic table at the gateway of the Route des Vins, four generations of Husser family cuisine paired with a deep local cellarMarlenheim

First-Michelin-starred 1936 hotel-restaurant at the start of the Route des Vins. Husser family kitchen, classical Alsatian gastronomy with a deep regional cellar.

Awards: Michelin 2026

30 Rue du General de Gaulle, 67520 Marlenheim, France →

Auberge du Cheval Blanc ★ 4.6

One-Michelin-star northern-Alsace coaching inn since 1822, chef Pascal Bastian plates classical-meets-modern cuisine inside a four-star Relais property paired with grower Alsace bottlesLembach

Pascal and Carole Bastian's 1822 coaching inn in northern Alsace, one Michelin star. Stuffed morels and Paris-Lembach dessert paired with grower-direct Alsace bottles.

Awards: Michelin 2025

4 Rue de Wissembourg, 67510 Lembach, France →

La Table du Gourmet ★ 4.7

Jean-Luc Brendel's one-Michelin-star (and Green Star) restaurant in a 16th-century half-timbered house in Riquewihr. Vegetable-led tasting menus and biodynamic Alsace pairings.

Awards: Michelin 2025, Michelin Green Star 2025, Gault Millau 2025

5 Rue de la 1ere Armee Francaise, 68340 Riquewihr, France →

Wistub Brenner ★ 4.4

Traditional Alsatian winstub in Colmar's Petite Venise quarter, choucroute, baeckeoffe and braised pork knuckle with a deep Alsace by-the-glass listPetite Venise Colmar

Riverside winstub in Petite Venise pouring an Alsace-only wine list alongside Papi Lucien's onion tart, choucroute and pork knuckle braised in Pinot Noir.

Wine Restaurants in Alsace, FAQ

When is the best time to visit Alsace for wine?

Peak wine-travel season in Alsace is spring through autumn, with harvest the standout window.

Do I need an appointment to taste at Alsace estates?

classified-growth and grand-cru estates require booking days to weeks ahead; smaller family domaines often take walk-ins midweek.

What hours do Alsace cellars and tasting rooms keep?

most estates open 10:00 to 17:00 by appointment, often closed Sunday and Monday.

How does tipping work at Alsace tastings?

tipping is not expected at tastings; buying a bottle from the cellar door is the customary thank-you.

What is the one wine to try in Alsace?

Ask the next local you meet what they would order. Alsace rewards trust.
