Iconic bottles of Alsace

Clos Sainte-Hune ★ 5.0

trimbachstill dry Alsace Riesling (single-vineyard)Rieslingapproachable from 8 years, cellar 20 to 40EUR 250 to 450 at retail

Trimbach's 1.67 hectare walled monopole inside Grand Cru Rosacker, family-declassified to Alsace AOC. The benchmark for dry, ageworthy Alsace Riesling.

Tasting notes: Trimbach's 1.67 hectare monopole inside Grand Cru Rosacker, declassified to Alsace AOC. Piercing dry Riesling that needs a decade to unwind into wax, citrus and Vosges granite salinity. Source: Trimbach producer page.

trimbachstill dry Alsace Riesling (Grand Cru blend)Rieslingapproachable from 5 years, cellar 15 to 25EUR 75 to 110 at retail

Tasting notes: Trimbach's flagship Riesling, a Geisberg and Osterberg Grand Cru parcel blend released after years of bottle age. Smoky, structured, ageworthy. Source: Trimbach producer page.

Signature Wines in Alsace, FAQ

When is the best time to visit Alsace for wine?

Peak wine-travel season in Alsace is spring through autumn, with harvest the standout window.

Do I need an appointment to taste at Alsace estates?

classified-growth and grand-cru estates require booking days to weeks ahead; smaller family domaines often take walk-ins midweek.

What hours do Alsace cellars and tasting rooms keep?

most estates open 10:00 to 17:00 by appointment, often closed Sunday and Monday.

How does tipping work at Alsace tastings?

tipping is not expected at tastings; buying a bottle from the cellar door is the customary thank-you.

What is the one wine to try in Alsace?

If you only open one bottle, open Clos Sainte-Hune by trimbach. It is the wine most associated with Alsace.
