Garganega around the world

23 editor-picked Garganega producers across 1 region.

A guide to Garganega worth seeking out, by region. We list the estates, how each region expresses the grape, and the bottles that show it best.

Garganega in Veneto

Pieropan ★ 4.9

Soave Superiore DOCG and Soave Classico DOCGarganega, Trebbiano di SoaveFounded 1880Pieropan family (Andrea and Dario Pieropan)Winemaker Andrea and Dario PieropanORGANIC CERTIFIEDSoave Classico

The reference producer for Soave since 1880, Pieropan produces single-vineyard bottlings La Rocca (volcanic tufa) and Calvarino (basalt and limestone) that define what world-class Garganega can achieve. Certified organic by Suolo e Salute since 2012.

Tasting: Visits and tastings at the historic Soave cellar; bookable via the website.

Signature wines: pieropan-soave-classico, pieropan-la-rocca, pieropan-calvarino

Via Camuzzoni 3, 37038 Soave, Verona, Italy → Book →

Inama Azienda Agricola ★ 4.7

Soave Superiore DOCG and Colli Berici DOCGarganega, Sauvignon Blanc, CarmenereFounded 1991Inama family (Giuseppe Inama and sons)Winemaker Matteo InamaSoave Classico

Founded by Giuseppe Inama in 1991, the estate spans Soave Classico hill vineyards and pioneered quality Carmenere in the Colli Berici DOC. The Vulcaia Soave from basalt soils is one of the appellation's most mineral expressions.

Tasting: Visits and tastings at San Bonifacio; Soave Classico and Colli Berici Carmenere tasting programmes.

Signature wines: inama-soave-classico-foscarino, inama-oratorio-san-lorenzo, inama-bradisismo

Via IV Novembre 29, 37047 San Bonifacio, Verona, Italy → Book →

Gini ★ 4.7

Soave Superiore DOCGGarganegaFounded 1600Gini family (Sandro and Claudio Gini)Winemaker Sandro and Claudio GiniSoave Classico

The Gini family has farmed on the volcanic hillsides of Monteforte d'Alpone since the 17th century. La Frosca and Col Foscarin are their benchmark single-vineyard Soaves from ancient Garganega vines on basalt-rich soils.

Tasting: Estate visits and tasting flights at Monteforte d'Alpone; booking required.

Signature wines: gini-soave-classico-la-frosca, gini-col-foscarin

Via Matteotti 42, 37032 Monteforte d'Alpone, Verona, Italy → Book →

Suavia ★ 4.7

Soave Superiore DOCGGarganega, Trebbiano di SoaveFounded 1982Tessari family (Alessandra and Meri Tessari)Winemaker Alessandra TessariSoave Classico

Sisters Alessandra and Meri Tessari run this compact estate in the village of Soave producing Classico and the single-cru Monte Carbonare from centenarian Garganega vines on volcanic tufa soils.

Tasting: Tasting visits by appointment in the village of Soave.

Signature wines: suavia-soave-classico, suavia-monte-carbonare, suavia-amedeo

Via Centro 14, 37038 Soave, Verona, Italy → Book →

Pra ★ 4.7

Soave Superiore DOCGGarganegaFounded 1983Graziano PraWinemaker Graziano PraORGANIC CERTIFIEDSoave Classico

Graziano Pra converted this Monteforte d'Alpone estate to organic viticulture and produces deeply terroir-driven Soave Classico Monte Grande from volcanic basalt and garnet-clay soils alongside the richer Otto bottling.

Tasting: Cellar visit and tasting at Monteforte d'Alpone by appointment.

Signature wines: pra-soave-classico-monte-grande, pra-otto

Via della Fontana 31, 37032 Monteforte d'Alpone, Verona, Italy → Book →

Cantina di Soave ★ 4.2

Soave Classico DOCGarganega, Trebbiano di Soave, Corvina, RondinellaFounded 1898Cooperative of 2,600 member growersWinemaker Cantina di Soave winemaking teamSoave Classico

The largest Soave cooperative, founded in 1898, representing 2,600 member growers across 6,000 hectares. Its premium Rocca Sveva line is entry-level access to the appellation; the visitor centre in town is walk-in friendly.

Tasting: Public tasting room open daily in Soave town; walk-in welcome.

Signature wines: cantina-soave-rocca-sveva

Via Vittorio Emanuele II 94, 37038 Soave, Verona, Italy → Book →

Roberto Anselmi ★ 4.8

Veneto IGTGarganega, ChardonnayFounded 1948Roberto AnselmiWinemaker Roberto AnselmiSoave Classico

Roberto Anselmi famously resigned from the Soave Consorzio and declassified his wines to Veneto IGT as a protest against commercial dilution of the appellation. His San Vincenzo, Capitel Croce and I Capitelli (sweet) remain defining Garganega benchmarks.

Tasting: Tasting visits at Monteforte d'Alpone by appointment.

Signature wines: anselmi-san-vincenzo, anselmi-capitel-croce, anselmi-i-capitelli

Via San Carlo 46, 37032 Monteforte d'Alpone, Verona, Italy → Book →

Cavalchina ★ 4.4

Bianco di Custoza DOCTrebbiano Toscano, Garganega, Cortese, Bianca Fernanda, Riesling ItalicoFounded 1974Piona familyWinemaker Piona familyCustoza

The Piona family estate at Sommacampagna is one of the benchmarks for Bianco di Custoza DOC, producing a crisp blend of Trebbiano, Garganega and Cortese on the morainic hills south of Lake Garda.

Tasting: Tasting visits at the Sommacampagna estate by appointment.

Signature wines: cavalchina-bianco-di-custoza, cavalchina-amedeo

Loc. Cavalchina 7, 37066 Sommacampagna, Verona, Italy → Book →

Fasoli Gino ★ 4.5

Soave Classico DOCGarganega, Trebbiano di Soave, CorvinaFounded 1924Fasoli familyWinemaker Fasoli familyICEASoave Classico

ICEA-certified organic estate at Colognola ai Colli producing Soave Classico and Valpolicella from certified-organic vineyards. A pioneer of organic viticulture in the Soave zone.

Tasting: Organic estate visits and tastings by appointment at Colognola ai Colli.

Signature wines: fasoli-gino-soave-classico-mael

Via C. Battisti 47, 37030 Colognola ai Colli, Verona, Italy → Book →

Monte Tondo ★ 4.4

Soave Superiore DOCGGarganega, Trebbiano di SoaveFounded 1969Magnabosco familyWinemaker Magnabosco familySoave Classico

The Magnabosco family's estate on the volcanic hillsides east of Soave Classico produces well-structured Soave Superiore DOCG from the Casette Foscarin vineyard on high-elevation basalt terraces.

Tasting: Tasting visits at the Soave estate by appointment.

Signature wines: monte-tondo-casette-foscarin

Via San Lorenzo 89, 37038 Soave, Verona, Italy → Book →

Gorgo ★ 4.4

Bianco di Custoza DOCGarganega, Trebbiano Toscano, Cortese, MerlotFounded 1966Custoza

A family estate on the glacial morainic amphitheater south of Lake Garda at Custoza. The estate specialises in crisp, food-friendly Bianco di Custoza DOC from Garganega, Trebbiano and Cortese.

Tasting: Tasting visits at the morainic estate in Custoza by appointment.

Signature wines: gorgo-bianco-di-custoza, gorgo-merlot-garda

Via Gorgo 17, 37067 Custoza di Valeggio sul Mincio, Verona, Italy → Book →

Corte Gardoni ★ 4.4

Bianco di Custoza DOCGarganega, Trebbiano Toscano, Cortese, Cabernet Sauvignon, MerlotFounded 1980Piccoli familyWinemaker Piccoli familyCustoza

The Piccoli family estate at Valeggio sul Mincio on the morainic rim of Lake Garda produces a fresh, saline Bianco di Custoza DOC alongside an interesting Garda Cabernet from south-shore hillside vineyards.

Tasting: Tasting visits at the Valeggio sul Mincio estate; bookable online.

Signature wines: corte-gardoni-bianco-di-custoza, corte-gardoni-le-fontane-garda-cabernet

Loc. Gardoni 5, 37067 Valeggio sul Mincio, Verona, Italy → Book →

Cantina di Custoza ★ 4.1

Bianco di Custoza DOCGarganega, Trebbiano Toscano, CorteseFounded 1958Cooperative of local growersWinemaker Cooperative winemaking teamCustoza

The Custoza cooperative founded in 1958 is the largest producer in the Bianco di Custoza DOC appellation. High-volume accessible wines from morainic soils at good prices; the walk-in shop is a tourist draw on the Garda circuit.

Tasting: Public shop and tasting room at Custoza; walk-in welcome.

Signature wines: cantina-custoza-bianco-di-custoza

Via Sommacampagna 3, 37060 Custoza di Sommacampagna, Verona, Italy → Book →

Cantine Campagnola ★ 4.3

Bardolino Superiore DOCGCorvina, Rondinella, Molinara, GarganegaFounded 1889Campagnola familyWinemaker Campagnola familyBardolino

Founded in 1889 at the Lake Garda waterfront in Bardolino, Cantine Campagnola is one of the appellation's oldest estates producing light, food-friendly Bardolino Superiore DOCG and a range of Valpolicella.

Tasting: Visits and tastings at the Bardolino cellar; daily opening.

Signature wines: campagnola-bardolino-classico, campagnola-valpolicella-classico

Via Agnella 9, 37011 Bardolino, Verona, Italy → Book →

Cantina del Castello Tasting Room ★ 4.5

Garganega, Trebbiano di SoaveWalk-ins welcomeSoave, Verona Province

In the heart of the medieval village of Soave at the foot of Scaliger Castle, Cantina del Castello has produced Soave Classico in the 13th-century Palazzo dei Conti Sambonifacio since the 1960s. The cellar is open Mon-Fri 8:00-18:30 and Sat 10:00-17:00 for tastings and retail.

Tip: Ask for the Pressoni single-vineyard cru - the volcanic basalt terroir character is unmistakable and shows what separates Soave Classico from the broader DOC.

Vicolo Corte Pittora 5, 37038 Soave (VR), Italy →

Villa Cordevigo Wine Relais Cellar Tasting ★ 4.6

Corvina, Corvinone, Rondinella, GarganegaAppointment onlyBardolino, Cavaion Veronese

The wine cellar tasting at Villa Cordevigo presents estate wines from Vigneti Villabella (Garda DOC and Valpolicella DOC). The wine and history experience package is curated for both hotel guests and day visitors. Set in the Cavaion Veronese hills with views over vineyards and olive groves.

Tip: The wine and history package pairs cellar tasting with a guided narrative of the villa and estate - book this format rather than standalone tasting for full context.

Localita Cordevigo snc, 37010 Cavaion Veronese (VR), Italy →

Vigneti Villabella Tasting Room ★ 4.6

Corvina, Garganega, Bardolino grapesAppointment onlyBardolino, Cavaion Veronese

Vigneti Villabella is the estate winery of Villa Cordevigo Wine Relais, producing Garda DOC and Valpolicella DOC wines from the surrounding vineyards and olive groves. Cellar tastings for hotel guests and day visitors. The estate also houses the five-star Villa Cordevigo Wine Relais and the one-Michelin-star Oseleta restaurant.

Tip: Request the full Villabella flight including the Bardolino Chiaretto rosato - underrated and highly food-friendly with the estate's kitchen.

Localita Cordevigo snc, 37010 Cavaion Veronese (VR), Italy →

Davide Spillare ★ 4.5

Soave Classico DOC / Lessini Durello DOCGarganega, DurellaSoave Classico, Verona Province

Young Garganega producer in the Soave Classico volcanic hillsides fermenting without additions and using extended skin contact on his Pico cuvee. A benchmark name for natural-leaning Soave.

Tip: Look for the Pico skin-contact Garganega and the Durella sparkling; rare bottles and small production mean you need to buy direct or via specialist importers.

Via Scandolara 7, 37030 Brognoligo di Monteforte d'Alpone, Italy →

Filippi ★ 4.5

Soave Classico DOCGarganegaSoave Classico, Castelcerino sub-zone

Filippo Filippi farms the steep Castelcerino hillside vineyards of Soave Classico using organically certified viticulture. His wines are made with native yeasts and minimal sulfur, exploring the volcanic and limestone soil variations across the Castelcerino, Montefoscarino and Turbiana sub-zones.

Tip: Look for the Castelcerino label: grown on the 1971 pergola Veronese trained vines on calcareous hillside soils. Very limited production.

Via Pavia 36, 37030 Castelcerino di Soave, Italy →

Giovanni Ederle ★ 4.3

Gambellara DOC / Gambellara Classico DOCGarganegaGambellara DOC, Vicenza Province

A family estate in Montebello Vicentino making Gambellara Classico from volcanic basalt soils at the base of the Berici Hills. Gambellara DOC is the little-known sibling of Soave, also Garganega-based but with distinct volcanic character; Ederle's Classico is among the most honest expressions of the appellation.

Tip: Gambellara Vin Santo (the dried-grape dessert wine version) is also produced by Ederle; a very rare style worth seeking at the estate.

Contrada Fontanelle 1, 36054 Montebello Vicentino, Italy →

Pieropan Soave Classico DOC ★ 4.3

Soave Classico DOCGarganega, Trebbiano di Soaveunder-EUR-25still-white

The benchmark entry-level Soave Classico from the region's most celebrated estate. Garganega-dominant, volcanic hillside character: white almond, lemon zest and golden apple with a long mineral finish. Remarkable value for a hillside wine from one of Italy's most serious white-wine producers.

Tip: The Soave Classico DOC (not the single-vineyard Calvarino or La Rocca) is the entry wine; still benefits from one to two years of cellaring.

Inama Soave Classico DOC ★ 4.2

Soave Classico DOCGarganegaunder-EUR-25still-white

Inama's entry Soave is made from estate Garganega grown on the volcanic hillsides of Soave Classico. Floral and fresh on the nose with notes of chamomile, citrus and almond; precise and clean on the palate. Inama is known for restoring volcanic hillside Garganega to critical prominence.

Tip: Pair with the regional frittura di lago (fried freshwater fish) from Lake Garda; a classic Veneto white-fish pairing.

Coffele Soave Classico DOC Ca' Visco ★ 4.3

Soave Classico DOCGarganega, Trebbiano di Soaveunder-EUR-25still-white

Coffele's Ca' Visco is a single-vineyard hillside Soave Classico grown on basalt volcanic soils above the valley floor. White peach, chamomile and mineral saline on the palate; the basalt gives an almost smoky mineral edge. One of the most thoughtful value expressions of volcanic Garganega in the Classico zone.

Tip: Ca' Visco shows remarkable evolution with three to five years of bottle age; the almond-and-mineral character deepens considerably.
