Estates to visit in Ribera del Duero

Bodegas Vega Sicilia ★ 5.0

DO Ribera del DueroTinto Fino, Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, Malbec, AlbilloFounded 1864Quintanilla de Onésimo

The Ribera del Duero estate founded in 1864, producing Unico after a minimum of ten years' ageing and the Valbuena 5 degree as an earlier-release sibling.

Tasting: Visits strictly by appointment; contact the estate directly.

Carretera N-122, km 323, 47359 Valbuena de Duero, Valladolid, Spain →

Bodegas Alión ★ 4.8

DO Ribera del DueroTinto FinoFounded 1991Quintanilla de Onésimo

The second estate of the Vega Sicilia group, founded in 1991, producing a 100% Tinto Fino wine aged in French oak with a modern, approachable style relative to its famous sibling Unico.

Tasting: Visits by appointment through the Vega Sicilia group.

Carretera Peñafiel-Encinas, km 2, 47316 Padilla de Duero, Valladolid, Spain →

Dominio de Pingus ★ 5.0

DO Ribera del DueroTinto FinoFounded 1995Biodynamic PracticingORGANIC CERTIFIEDLa Horra

Peter Sisseck's cult estate in La Horra, producing Pingus from a single old-vine parcel and the more accessible Flor de Pingus and PSI from older purchased fruit across the appellation.

La Horra, 09311 La Horra, Burgos, Spain →

Tinto Pesquera ★ 4.8

DO Ribera del DueroTinto FinoFounded 1972Pesquera de Duero

Alejandro Fernandez's estate that brought global attention to Ribera del Duero in the 1980s, producing Tinto Pesquera Crianza, Reserva, Gran Reserva and the prestige Janus label from old Tinto Fino vines.

Tasting: Visits and tastings by prior arrangement at the estate.

Calle Real 2, 47315 Pesquera de Duero, Valladolid, Spain →

Bodegas Aalto ★ 4.8

DO Ribera del DueroTinto FinoFounded 1999Winemaker Mariano GarcíaQuintanilla de Onésimo

Founded in 1999 at Quintanilla de Arriba by former Vega Sicilia winemaker Mariano García and Javier Zaccagnini, producing single-vineyard PS and the Aalto flagship from old Tinto Fino vines.

Tasting: Visits by appointment.

Paraje Vallejo de Carril s/n, 47360 Quintanilla de Arriba, Valladolid, Spain → Book →

Bodegas Mauro ★ 4.7

VT Castilla y LeónTinto Fino, Garnacha Tinta, SyrahFounded 1980Sardón de Duero

Founded in 1980 by Mariano García at Tudela del Duero, producing the Mauro, Mauro VS and Terreus cuvées as VT Castilla y Leon because some vineyards fall outside the DO boundary, with 90 hectares under vine averaging 35 years.

Carretera de Villabañez km 1, 47320 Tudela del Duero, Valladolid, Spain →

Dominio del Águila ★ 4.8

DO Ribera del DueroTinto Fino, Albillo Mayor, Garnacha TintaFounded 2010ORGANIC CERTIFIEDLa Horra

Jorge Monzón's certified-organic estate at La Aguilera producing Pícaro del Águila rosé, Dominio del Águila and single-parcel Peñas Aladas from pre-phylloxera old vines.

La Aguilera, 09317 La Aguilera, Burgos, Spain →

Bodegas Emilio Moro ★ 4.7

DO Ribera del DueroTinto FinoFounded 1987Pesquera de Duero

A four-generation family estate at Pesquera de Duero founded commercially in 1987, producing the Emilio Moro Crianza, the Malleolus range and single-vineyard Malleolus de Valderramiro and Malleolus de Sanchomartín.

Tasting: Wine tourism with visits, tastings, restaurant and wine bar on site.

Tip: Book the on-site restaurant El Rincón de Emilio for local roast lamb after the cellar tour.

Carretera Peñafiel-Valoria s/n, 47315 Pesquera de Duero, Valladolid, Spain → Book →

Bodegas Protos ★ 4.6

DO Ribera del DueroTinto Fino, Albillo MayorFounded 1927Peñafiel

Founded in 1927 as a cooperative under Peñafiel Castle, Protos is the historic anchor of the appellation and the region's most-visited winery, housed in a spectacular Richard Rogers-designed cellar opened in 2008.

Tasting: The most-visited winery in Ribera del Duero, with guided tours open to the public since 2009; book via enoturismo@bodegasprotos.com or +34 659 843 463.

Tip: Combine the cellar tour with a visit to the castle-top Museo Provincial del Vino above.

C/ Bodegas Protos 24-28, 47300 Peñafiel, Valladolid, Spain → Book →

Pago de Carraovejas ★ 4.8

DO Ribera del DueroTinto Fino, Cabernet Sauvignon, MerlotFounded 1987Peñafiel

A landmark estate near Peñafiel famous for its Pago de Carraovejas Crianza and Reserva and the single-vineyard El Anejon, with the Michelin-starred Ambivium restaurant drawing visitors from across Castile.

Tasting: Wine tourism and cellar visits by appointment; on-site Ambivium restaurant holds a Michelin star.

Camino Carraovejas s/n, 47316 Peñafiel, Valladolid, Spain →

Bodegas Arzuaga Navarro ★ 4.6

DO Ribera del DueroTinto Fino, Cabernet Sauvignon, MerlotFounded 1993Quintanilla de Onésimo

A family estate and hospitality complex at Quintanilla de Onesimo, producing the Arzuaga Crianza, Reserva, Gran Arzuaga and single-vineyard Fan d'Or, with a hotel and spa on the vineyard.

Tasting: Full wine tourism complex with tastings, hotel, spa and on-site restaurant.

Carretera N-122, km 325, 47350 Quintanilla de Onésimo, Valladolid, Spain →

Hacienda Monasterio ★ 4.7

DO Ribera del DueroTinto Fino, Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, Cabernet Franc, MalbecFounded 1991Pesquera de Duero

A classic estate near Pesquera de Duero founded in 1991, with veteran consultant winemaker Peter Sisseck shaping the Hacienda Monasterio and Reserva Especial from a diverse five-variety blend.

Carretera Peñafiel-Pesquera km 4, 47315 Pesquera de Duero, Valladolid, Spain →

Bodegas Félix Callejo ★ 4.6

DO Ribera del DueroTinto FinoORGANIC CERTIFIEDPedrosa de Duero

A certified organic estate at Sotillo de la Ribera in Burgos province, where José Félix and daughter Noelia craft village-and single-vineyard Tinto Fino wines from high-altitude limestone soils at 830 to 930 metres.

Avenida del Cid km 16, 09441 Sotillo de la Ribera, Burgos, Spain →

Bodegas Hermanos Sastre ★ 4.6

DO Ribera del DueroTinto FinoFounded 1992La Horra

A La Horra estate founded by the Sastre brothers in 1992, producing the Roble, Crianza, Viña Sastre Reserva and single-parcel Pago de Santa Cruz from old Tinto Fino vines in the Burgos sector.

Camino de la Rábida s/n, 09311 La Horra, Burgos, Spain →

Goyo García Viadero ★ 4.7

DO Ribera del DueroTinto Fino, Albillo MayorFounded 2001Biodynamic PracticingORGANIC CERTIFIEDNaturalLa Horra

A natural-wine pioneer in the Burgos sector working with old pre-phylloxera Tinto Fino and Albillo Mayor vines, producing low-intervention wines with minimal sulfur under the Villalbilla and other labels.

Villalbilla de Gumiel, 09314 Villalbilla de Gumiel, Burgos, Spain →

Bodegas Cepa 21 ★ 4.5

DO Ribera del DueroTinto FinoFounded 2001Pesquera de Duero

The Moro family's second estate, founded in 2001 at Pesquera de Duero, focused on younger-vine Tinto Fino with the Cepa 21 and Hito entry labels and the premium Malabrigo from selected old parcels.

Carretera Renedo-Pesquera km 35, 47315 Pesquera de Duero, Valladolid, Spain →

Dominio de Atauta ★ 4.8

DO Ribera del DueroTinto Fino, Albillo MayorFounded 2000ORGANIC CERTIFIEDSoria Sub-zone

Specialists in extreme-altitude old-vine Tinto Fino from the Soria sub-zone at Atauta, where pre-phylloxera ungrafted vines at over 1000 metres produce the Llanos del Almendro and single-parcel La Mala cuvées.

Calle Real 21, 42312 Atauta, Soria, Spain →

Bodegas Carmelo Rodero ★ 4.5

A Pedrosa de Duero family estate producing the Carmelo Rodero Crianza, Reserva and 9 Meses, as well as TSM from old vines, with the founding family still at the helm after more than three decades.

Calle Las Escuelas 12, 09314 Pedrosa de Duero, Burgos, Spain →

Vineyards in Ribera del Duero, FAQ

When is the best time to visit Ribera del Duero for wine?

Peak wine-travel season in Ribera del Duero is spring through autumn, with harvest the standout window.

Do I need an appointment to taste at Ribera del Duero estates?

classified-growth and grand-cru estates require booking days to weeks ahead; smaller family domaines often take walk-ins midweek.

What hours do Ribera del Duero cellars and tasting rooms keep?

most estates open 10:00 to 17:00 by appointment, often closed Sunday and Monday.

How does tipping work at Ribera del Duero tastings?

tipping is not expected at tastings; buying a bottle from the cellar door is the customary thank-you.

What is the one wine to try in Ribera del Duero?

Ask the next local you meet what they would order. Ribera del Duero rewards trust.
