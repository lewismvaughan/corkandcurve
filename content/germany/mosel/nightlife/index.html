Wine Bars Late in Mosel

Weinstube Kesselstatt ★ 4.5

20 by the glassTrier (Altstadt)

Historic estate Weinstube of Reichsgraf von Kesselstatt in the heart of Trier, pouring the family's Mosel, Saar and Ruwer Rieslings until midnight.

The list: The estate tavern of Reichsgraf von Kesselstatt, pouring the family's Mosel, Saar and Ruwer Rieslings in a 250-year-old palace beside the Liebfrauenkirche

Tip: Sit in the garden under the cathedral and order a flight of Kesselstatt's Scharzhofberger Saar Rieslings beside the dry Mosel wines.

Liebfrauenstrasse 10, 54290 Trier, Germany →

Weinsinnig ★ 4.3

25 by the glassTrier (Altstadt)

Relaxed central-Trier wine bar focused on Mosel growers, open evenings Tuesday to Saturday with an uncomplicated, well-chosen by-the-glass list.

The list: A small, knowledgeable wine bar in the centre of Trier with a list built mostly on Mosel growers, poured by the glass with light plates

Tip: The by-the-glass selection rotates through small Mosel estates; ask the staff to steer you to something off the main list.

Palaststrasse 12, 54290 Trier, Germany →

Die Mosel Vinothek & Winebar ★ 4.3

30 by the glassTraben-Trarbach

Wine bar and vinothek in an 1890 merchant's villa on the Traben-Trarbach riverbank, pouring around 150 characteristic Mosel wines into the evening.

The list: Around 150 characteristic Mosel wines in a listed 1890 wine-merchant's villa on the riverbank, with a wine bar pouring by the glass into the evening

Tip: Friday to Sunday it opens from early afternoon; grab a riverside table and work through the Mosel-only by-the-glass selection.

Rissbacher Strasse 13, 56841 Traben-Trarbach, Germany →

Tresor Wine & Bar ★ 4.1

20 by the glassBernkastel-Kues (Markt)

Evening wine bar inside the Ratskeller on Bernkastel-Kues' market square, pouring Mosel Riesling by the glass from late afternoon.

The list: The wine bar of the Ratskeller in the old town hall on Bernkastel's market square, pouring Mosel wines by the glass from early evening

Tip: Open from 5pm Tuesday to Sunday right on the famous Bernkastel market square; ideal first stop before dinner in the old town.

Markt 30, 54470 Bernkastel-Kues, Germany →

Beim Weinbauer ★ 4.0

18 by the glassCochem

Wine and Sekt bar in a 16th-century Cochem house, pairing Mosel wines with small plates and tapas in the evening.

The list: A wine and sparkling-wine bar in one of Cochem's oldest 16th-century houses, pairing Mosel wines and Sekt with small plates and tapas

Tip: The 16th-century house is as much the draw as the list; order the tapas-style plates to graze through several Mosel pours.

Oberbachstrasse 56, 56812 Cochem, Germany →

Weinwirtschaft Friedrich-Wilhelm ★ 4.1

22 by the glassTrier (Altstadt)

Trier wine bar and restaurant pouring a broad selection from the city's historic estates, including the Bischoefliche Weingueter, into the evening.

The list: A wine bar and restaurant pouring a large selection from the Bischoefliche Weingueter, the Friedrich-Wilhelm-Gymnasium estate and the Rinke winery, open into the evening

Tip: The list leans on Trier's historic church-linked estates; a good place to taste the Bischoefliche Weingueter range by the glass.

Weberbach 75, 54290 Trier, Germany →

Burgblickhotel Weinbar ★ 4.0

20 by the glassBernkastel-Kues (Kues)

Modern hotel wine bar in Bernkastel-Kues pouring established Mosel estates alongside emerging young growers, with a terrace facing Burg Landshut.

The list: A modern hotel wine bar pouring both established Mosel names like Dr. Loosen and emerging young growers, with a castle-view terrace

Tip: Ask for the newcomer-grower flight; the bar deliberately balances classic estates against up-and-coming Mosel names.

Goethestrasse 29, 54470 Bernkastel-Kues, Germany →

Listening Bars in Mosel

Wirtshaus Zur Glocke ★ 3.9

12 by the glassTrier (Altstadt)

Cosy, low-lit old-Trier tavern pouring regional wine with hearty food until midnight, a quiet conversational room rather than a loud bar.

The list: A snug, old-school Trier tavern serving regional wine and hearty food in a low-lit, conversational room open late every night

Tip: This is a talking-and-drinking room, not a music venue; come for the unhurried, low-lit old-Trier atmosphere over local wine.

Glockenstrasse 12, 54290 Trier, Germany →

Candle Lit in Mosel

Weinstube Spitzhaeuschen ★ 4.4

15 by the glassBernkastel-Kues (Altstadt)

Tiny, candle-lit wine tavern in a leaning 1416 cottage off Bernkastel's market square, pouring the Schmitz family's Mosel Rieslings.

The list: The wine tavern of the Schmitz family vineyard inside a leaning 1416 cottage, pouring over 50 Mosel Rieslings and reds in a tiny candle-lit room

Tip: The crooked 1416 pointed cottage is one of the most photographed buildings on the Mosel; squeeze into the upstairs nook with a glass of estate Riesling.

Karlstrasse 13, 54470 Bernkastel-Kues, Germany →

Alte Zunftscheune ★ 4.1

14 by the glassTraben-Trarbach

Atmospheric evening wine room and restaurant in a historic Traben-Trarbach guild barn, pouring local Mosel wines from 5pm.

The list: A warmly atmospheric evening restaurant and wine room in old Traben-Trarbach, pouring local Mosel wines from 5pm Tuesday to Saturday

Tip: Book ahead; the candle-lit guild-barn room fills fast on weekend evenings in the heart of Traben-Trarbach.

Neue Rathausstrasse 15, 56841 Traben-Trarbach, Germany →

Doctor Weinstube ★ 4.0

16 by the glassBernkastel-Kues (Altstadt)

Half-timbered hotel Weinstube in Bernkastel's old town, named for the Bernkasteler Doctor vineyard and pouring Mosel Riesling in a beamed candle-lit room.

The list: A timber-framed hotel Weinstube in Bernkastel's old town named for the famous Bernkasteler Doctor vineyard, pouring Mosel Riesling in a beamed candle-lit room

Tip: Order a glass of the namesake Bernkasteler Doctor, one of the Mosel's most storied single vineyards, in the half-timbered dining room.

Hebegasse 5, 54470 Bernkastel-Kues, Germany →

Brueckenkeller ★ 3.9

14 by the glassBernkastel-Kues (riverside)

Snug vaulted cellar wine tavern by the Mosel bridge in Bernkastel-Kues, pouring local Riesling with views toward Burg Landshut.

The list: A cosy cellar wine tavern by the Mosel bridge with views to Burg Landshut, pouring local Riesling in a low, candle-lit vaulted room

Tip: Grab a riverside table at dusk for the floodlit Burg Landshut view, then move into the vaulted cellar for a nightcap Riesling.

Gestade 4, 54470 Bernkastel-Kues, Germany →

Weingut Stein - Haus Waldfrieden ★ 4.1

12 by the glassAlf and Bullay

Ulli Stein's candle-lit tasting house above Alf, hosting evening estate wine tastings with live music and cultural events for small groups.

The list: Ulli Stein's atmospheric tasting house above Alf, the venue for estate wine tastings and live-music and cultural evenings for small groups

Tip: Evenings here pair Stein's dry slate Rieslings and rare Elbling with live music; tastings run for groups, so call ahead to join one.

Brautrockstrasse 40, 56859 Bullay, Germany →

Van Volxem Panorama Lounge ★ 4.4

12 by the glassWiltingen (Saar)

Architect-designed vinotheque and glass panorama lounge of the Van Volxem estate above the Saar, with counter tastings of mineral Saar Riesling.

The list: The architect-designed vinotheque and panorama lounge of a leading Saar estate, with a lift up to a glass lounge overlooking the Saar loop and counter tastings of estate Riesling

Tip: Ride the lift to the glass panorama lounge for the Saar-loop view, then taste the estate's mineral Wiltingen Rieslings at the counter.

Zum Schlossberg 347, 54459 Wiltingen, Germany →

DEINHARD's Hotel Lounge ★ 4.0

16 by the glassBernkastel-Kues (riverside)

Riverside hotel lounge and bar at DEINHARD's in Bernkastel-Kues, serving Mosel wines and the hotel's own traditional-method Sekt.

The list: The riverside lounge and bar of DEINHARD's hotel on the Bernkastel market house, serving Mosel wines and the hotel's own traditional-method Sekt

Tip: Settle into the riverside lounge with a glass of the house Sekt before dinner; the hotel makes its own sparkling in the cellar below.

Gestade 12, 54470 Bernkastel-Kues, Germany →

Weingut von Othegraven Vinothek ★ 4.2

14 by the glassKanzem (Saar)

Estate vinothek of the 16th-century von Othegraven house in Kanzem, now owned by Guenther Jauch, pouring Grosse Lage Saar Riesling.

The list: The estate vinothek of a 16th-century Saar house, now run by Guenther Jauch, pouring its Grosse Lage Riesling from the Kanzemer Altenberg and Wiltinger sites

Tip: From May to October the vinothek opens Saturdays too; taste the Kanzemer Altenberg Riesling at one of the Saar's most beautiful estates.

Weinstrasse 1, 54441 Kanzem, Germany →

Late Tastings in Mosel

Mosel-Vinothek (St. Nicholas Hospital) ★ 4.4

100 by the glassBernkastel-Kues (Kues)

The world's largest Mosel-Riesling vinotheque, in the vaulted St Nicholas Hospital cellars in Bernkastel-Kues, with self-pour tastings of over 100 wines.

The list: The largest vinotheque for Mosel Riesling in the world, in the vaulted cellars of the St Nicholas Hospital, where over 100 regional wines can be tasted

Tip: Buy a tasting card and self-pour your way through more than 100 Mosel wines in the medieval hospital cellars; arrive mid-afternoon to avoid the tour crowds.

Cusanusstrasse 2, 54470 Bernkastel-Kues, Germany →

Weinhaus Hieronimi ★ 4.0

20 by the glassCochem

Family wine cellar, cafe and Mosel garden in Cochem, with evening tastings of estate Riesling and other Mosel wines overlooking the river.

The list: A wine cellar, wine cafe and Mosel garden in Cochem where the family's own Riesling and other Mosel wines are tasted with a view over the river

Tip: The riverside garden is the spot for an early-evening estate tasting; the cellar pours the family's own Cochem wines.

Stadionstrasse 1, 56812 Cochem, Germany →

Riverside estate vinothek in Piesport on the Mosel cycle path, pouring the family's Piesporter Goldtroepfchen Rieslings for tasting.

The list: An idyllic estate vinothek directly on the Mosel and the cycle path in Piesport, pouring the family's Goldtroepfchen Rieslings for tasting

Seasonal grower-run pop-up taverns in courtyards and cellars around Bernkastel, pouring the family's own wine with home cooking in spring and autumn.

The list: Seasonal pop-up grower taverns set up in winegrowers' courtyards and cellars in and around Bernkastel, pouring the family's own wine with simple home cooking

Tip: A straw wreath or bouquet over a grower's door means the family is pouring their own wine that night; these Strausswirtschaften open in spring and autumn.

Nightlife in Mosel, FAQ

When is the best time to visit Mosel for wine?

Peak wine-travel season in Mosel is spring through autumn, with harvest the standout window.

Do I need an appointment to taste at Mosel estates?

classified-growth and grand-cru estates require booking days to weeks ahead; smaller family domaines often take walk-ins midweek.

What hours do Mosel cellars and tasting rooms keep?

most estates open 10:00 to 17:00 by appointment, often closed Sunday and Monday.

How does tipping work at Mosel tastings?

tipping is not expected at tastings; buying a bottle from the cellar door is the customary thank-you.

What is the one wine to try in Mosel?

Ask the next local you meet what they would order. Mosel rewards trust.
