Estates to visit in Mosel

Weingut Joh. Jos. Prum ★ 4.9

VDP.Grosse Lage MoselRieslingFounded 1911Prum family (Katharina Prum)Wehlen

The most famous Riesling estate in Wehlen, known worldwide for long-lived, reductive, off-dry wines from the Wehlener Sonnenuhr. Family-run and dedicated entirely to Riesling on blue Devonian slate.

Tasting: Tastings by prior appointment only; contact the estate office by email to arrange a visit.

Uferallee 19, 54470 Bernkastel-Wehlen, Germany → Book →

Weingut Dr. Loosen ★ 4.8

VDP.Grosse Lage MoselRieslingFounded 1988Ernst LoosenWinemaker Ernst LoosenBernkastel-Kues

Ernst Loosen's estate holds parcels of ungrafted old vines in many of the Mittelmosel's grand crus, from Wehlener Sonnenuhr to Erdener Pralat and the Bernkasteler Lay. A leading global ambassador for Mosel Riesling.

Tasting: Wine tastings and cellar-door sales Monday to Saturday 10:00 to 17:00 by appointment at St. Johannishof.

St. Johannishof, 54470 Bernkastel-Kues, Germany → Book →

Weingut Fritz Haag ★ 4.8

VDP.Grosse Lage MoselRieslingFounded 1605Haag family (Oliver Haag)Winemaker Oliver HaagBrauneberg

A benchmark estate on the south-facing Brauneberger Juffer and Juffer-Sonnenuhr, run by Oliver Haag. Crystalline, racy Rieslings with documented roots back to 1605.

Tasting: Tastings and sales by appointment at the Dusemonder Hof estate in Brauneberg.

Dusemonder Strasse 44, 54472 Brauneberg, Germany → Book →

Weingut Willi Schaefer ★ 4.8

VDP.Grosse Lage MoselRieslingSchaefer family (Christoph Schaefer)Winemaker Christoph SchaeferGraach

A tiny, revered grower in Graach producing precise, mineral Rieslings from the Domprobst and Himmelreich sites. Hand-worked steep slate, classic fruity Pradikat styles.

Tasting: Small family estate with no fixed opening hours; visits and tastings by appointment, typically April to July.

Hauptstrasse 130, 54470 Graach an der Mosel, Germany → Book →

Weingut Selbach-Oster ★ 4.6

Mosel PradikatsweinRieslingFounded 1661Selbach family (Johannes Selbach)Winemaker Johannes SelbachZeltingen-Rachtig

A long-established family estate working steep parcels in Zeltingen, Wehlen, Graach and Bernkastel. Known for old-vine, hand-harvested Rieslings and characterful dry and off-dry bottlings.

Tasting: Tastings at the Selbach Vinothek, Gansfelder Strasse 20; Saturday 11:00 to 17:00 and Sundays and holidays by appointment.

Gansfelder Strasse 20, 54492 Zeltingen-Rachtig, Germany → Book →

Weingut Markus Molitor ★ 4.7

VDP.Grosse Lage MoselRiesling, Spatburgunder (Pinot Noir), Weissburgunder (Pinot Blanc)Founded 1984Markus MolitorWinemaker Markus MolitorWehlen

An ambitious estate spanning the Mosel and Saar with one of the largest collections of top sites in the region, plus serious Mosel Pinot Noir. Famous for its star-coded labelling of dry, off-dry and sweet styles.

Tasting: Vinothek open Monday to Friday 10:00 to 17:00, weekends and holidays by appointment.

Haus Klosterberg, 54470 Bernkastel-Wehlen, Germany → Book →

Weingut Egon Muller - Scharzhof ★ 5.0

VDP.Grosse Lage MoselRieslingEgon MullerWinemaker Egon MullerSaar

The most prestigious estate of the Saar, owner of the heart of the Scharzhofberg. Its Trockenbeerenauslese and Auslese rank among the most expensive and ageworthy white wines in the world.

Tasting: Visits by appointment only; the estate sells primarily through auction and allocation.

Scharzhof, 54459 Wiltingen, Germany → Book →

Weingut Van Volxem ★ 4.7

VDP.Grosse Lage MoselRieslingFounded 2000Roman NiewodniczanskiSaar

A modern Saar powerhouse revived by Roman Niewodniczanski, reclaiming historic grand-cru sites and championing dry, full-bodied, old-vine Riesling from a striking new winery above Wiltingen.

Tasting: Vinothek and wine sales open Tuesday to Sunday 11:00 to 18:00.

Zum Schlossberg 347, 54459 Wiltingen, Germany → Book →

Weingut Forstmeister Geltz-Zilliken ★ 4.7

VDP.Grosse Lage MoselRieslingFounded 1742Zilliken family (Dorothee Zilliken)Winemaker Dorothee ZillikenSaar

A historic Saar estate centred on the Saarburger Rausch and Ockfener Bockstein, producing piercingly mineral, age-worthy Rieslings matured in a famously deep slate cellar.

Tasting: Tastings and sales by appointment; the estate is known for its deep, cold Saarburg cellar.

Heckingstrasse 20, 54439 Saarburg, Germany → Book →

Weingut Peter Lauer ★ 4.7

VDP.Grosse Lage MoselRieslingLauer family (Florian Lauer)Winemaker Florian LauerSaar

A fifth-generation Ayl estate famous for its barrel-fermented, dry-tasting Fass-numbered Rieslings from the Ayler Kupp. Vegan production and a cult following for its precise, gastronomic Saar style.

Tasting: Tastings at the estate and at the attached Weinhotel Ayler Kupp restaurant; by arrangement.

Trierer Strasse 49, 54441 Ayl, Germany → Book →

Weingut von Hovel ★ 4.5

VDP.Grosse Lage MoselRieslingvon Kunow family (Maximilian von Kunow)Winemaker Maximilian von KunowSaar

A Saar estate at Oberemmel with sole ownership of the Oberemmeler Hutte monopole and parcels in the Scharzhofberg. Maximilian von Kunow makes classically styled, transparent Saar Riesling.

Tasting: Office open Monday to Friday mornings; tastings by arrangement at the Oberemmel estate.

Agritiusstrasse 5-6, 54329 Konz-Oberemmel, Germany → Book →

Hofgut Falkenstein ★ 4.6

Mosel PradikatsweinRieslingFounded 1981Weber family (Erich and Johannes Weber)Winemaker Johannes WeberSaar

A cult Saar grower making spontaneously fermented, barrel-aged Rieslings labelled in an old-fashioned cask-numbered style. Dry-leaning, transparent wines from sites around Niedermennig and Krettnach.

Tasting: Small estate; tastings and sales by appointment at the Falkensteinerhof.

Falkensteinerhof 1, 54329 Konz-Niedermennig, Germany → Book →

Weingut Maximin Grunhaus ★ 4.8

VDP.Grosse Lage MoselRiesling, Weissburgunder (Pinot Blanc), Spatburgunder (Pinot Noir)von Schubert family (Maximin von Schubert)Winemaker Maximin von SchubertRuwer

A monastic-rooted Ruwer estate whose three monopole vineyards, Abtsberg, Herrenberg and Brudersberg, surround the manor. Filigree, long-lived Rieslings with documented winegrowing back to the 10th century.

Tasting: Open Monday to Friday 08:00 to 17:00; Saturdays 10:00 to 16:00 from May to October, and by appointment.

Maximin Grunhaus 1, 54318 Mertesdorf, Germany → Book →

Weingut Karthauserhof ★ 4.7

VDP.Grosse Lage MoselRieslingFounded 1335Christoph Tyrell and Albert BehlerRuwer

A historic Ruwer estate founded by Carthusian monks in 1335, owner of the Eitelsbacher Karthauserhofberg monopole. Distinctive neck-only labels and racy, mineral Riesling.

Tasting: Open Monday to Friday 08:30 to 17:00; Saturdays April to October 10:00 to 15:00, and by appointment.

Karthauserhof 1, 54292 Trier-Eitelsbach, Germany → Book →

Weingut Reichsgraf von Kesselstatt ★ 4.5

VDP.Grosse Lage MoselRiesling, Weissburgunder (Pinot Blanc)Reh family (managing director Dr. Karsten Weyand)Ruwer

A large historic estate with holdings across the Mosel, Saar and Ruwer, based at the listed Schloss Marienlay above the Ruwer. Riesling from grand crus including Scharzhofberg, Josephshofer and Kaseler Nies'chen.

Tasting: Tastings at Schloss Marienlay; office hours Monday to Thursday to 16:45, Friday to 13:00.

Schlossgut Marienlay, 54317 Morscheid, Germany → Book →

Weingut Heymann-Lowenstein ★ 4.7

VDP.Grosse Lage MoselRieslingLowenstein family (Reinhard Lowenstein, Sarah Lowenstein)Winemaker Reinhard LowensteinTerrassenmosel

A pioneer of dry, terroir-driven Lower Mosel Riesling from the terraced Winninger Uhlen and Rottgen sites. Powerful, parcel-specific, spontaneously fermented wines on the Terrassenmosel.

Tasting: Tastings and sales at the Winningen estate by appointment.

Bahnhofstrasse 10, 56333 Winningen, Germany → Book →

Weingut Knebel ★ 4.6

VDP.Grosse Lage MoselRieslingKnebel family (Matthias Knebel)Winemaker Matthias KnebelTerrassenmosel

A leading Terrassenmosel grower with prime parcels in the Winninger Rottgen, Uhlen and Hamm. Matthias Knebel makes precise, mineral, mostly dry Riesling from the terraced Lower Mosel.

Tasting: Open Monday to Saturday by prior telephone appointment at the Winningen estate.

August-Horch-Strasse 24, 56333 Winningen, Germany → Book →

Weingut Clemens Busch ★ 4.7

VDP.Grosse Lage MoselRieslingBusch family (Clemens and Florian Busch)Winemaker Clemens BuschBiodynamic PracticingORGANIC CERTIFIEDMittelmosel

A biodynamic pioneer in Punderich working the single Marienburg slope as separate blue, red and grey-slate parcels. Organic since 1984 and certified biodynamic since 2015; characterful, often skin-influenced dry Riesling.

Tasting: Tastings and sales by appointment at the Punderich estate.

Kirchstrasse 37, 56862 Punderich, Germany → Book →

Weingut Reinhold Haart ★ 4.7

VDP.Grosse Lage MoselRieslingHaart family (Theo and Johannes Haart)Winemaker Johannes HaartPiesport

The leading estate of Piesport, with the lion's share of the perfumed Goldtropfchen. Family vineyard ownership documented since 1337; aromatic, peach-and-citrus Riesling of great finesse.

Tasting: Tastings by appointment at the riverside estate in Piesport.

Ausoniusufer 18, 54498 Piesport, Germany → Book →

Weingut Schloss Lieser ★ 4.7

VDP.Grosse Lage MoselRieslingHaag family (Thomas Haag)Winemaker Thomas HaagMittelmosel

Thomas Haag's estate at the castle village of Lieser, with parcels in the Brauneberger Juffer, Wehlener Sonnenuhr and Niederberg Helden. Multiple-time German winery of the year for its precise Rieslings.

Tasting: Vinothek tastings and sales at the estate in Lieser; visits by arrangement.

Am Markt 1-5, 54470 Lieser, Germany → Book →

Weingut Immich-Batterieberg ★ 4.6

Mosel PradikatsweinRieslingGernot Kollmann and partnersWinemaker Gernot KollmannMittelmosel

One of the oldest estates on the Mosel, revived by Gernot Kollmann around the old-vine Enkircher sites of Batterieberg, Steffensberg and Ellergrub. Dry, spontaneously fermented, low-intervention Riesling.

Tasting: Tastings and sales by appointment at the estate in Enkirch.

Im Alten Tal 2, 56850 Enkirch, Germany → Book →

Weingut Vollenweider ★ 4.5

Mosel PradikatsweinRieslingFounded 2000Moritz Hoffmann (estate founded by Daniel Vollenweider)Winemaker Moritz HoffmannMittelmosel

A boutique estate founded by Swiss-born Daniel Vollenweider and centred on the steep old-vine Wolfer Goldgrube, now led by Moritz Hoffmann. Concentrated, classically fruity Mosel Riesling from the Traben-Trarbach area.

Tasting: Small estate; tastings and sales by appointment in Traben-Trarbach.

Wolfer Weg 53, 56841 Traben-Trarbach, Germany → Book →

Weingut Weiser-Kunstler ★ 4.6

Mosel PradikatsweinRieslingFounded 2005Konstantin Weiser and Alexandra KunstlerWinemaker Konstantin WeiserMittelmosel

A sought-after small grower working steep, organically farmed parcels in the Enkircher Ellergrub and Trabener Gaispfad. Detailed, low-intervention Riesling in dry and fruity styles.

Tasting: Small estate; tastings and sales by appointment in Traben-Trarbach.

Wilhelmstrasse 11, 56841 Traben-Trarbach, Germany → Book →

Weingut Joh. Jos. Christoffel Erben ★ 4.5

Mosel PradikatsweinRieslingRobert EymaelUrzig and Erden

A storied Urzig label held by the Christoffel family for four centuries and now made and distributed by Robert Eymael of Monchhof. Classic, delicately sweet Riesling from the Urziger Wurzgarten and Erdener Treppchen.

Tasting: Tastings at the Monchhof Vinothek, where both estates' wines can be tasted and bought.

Monchhof, 54539 Urzig, Germany → Book →

Weingut Monchhof - Robert Eymael ★ 4.4

Mosel PradikatsweinRieslingEymael family (Robert Eymael)Urzig and Erden

One of the oldest estates in the Mosel, with records back to the Cistercian abbey of Himmerod in 1177. Robert Eymael makes traditional fruity Riesling from the Urziger Wurzgarten and Erdener sites.

Tasting: Vinothek tastings of both Monchhof and Joh. Jos. Christoffel Erben wines; visits by arrangement.

Monchhof, 54539 Urzig, Germany → Book →

Weingut Dr. Hermann ★ 4.4

Mosel PradikatsweinRieslingHermann family (Christian Hermann)Winemaker Christian HermannUrzig and Erden

An Erden family estate with prime holdings in the steep Erdener Pralat and Treppchen and the Urziger Wurzgarten. Classic fruity-style Mosel Riesling across the Pradikat ladder.

Tasting: Tastings and sales by appointment at the riverside estate in Erden.

Uferallee 25, 54492 Erden, Germany → Book →

Weingut S.A. Prum ★ 4.4

A Wehlen estate housed in a 1920s reform-style villa, with parcels in the Wehlener Sonnenuhr and Graacher Himmelreich. Now led by Saskia Prum, producing classic and dry Mosel Riesling.

Tasting: Tastings and sales at the listed villa estate in Wehlen; visits by arrangement.

Uferallee 25, 54470 Bernkastel-Wehlen, Germany → Book →

Weingut Studert-Prum ★ 4.3

A long-established Wehlen family estate, the Maximinhof, with parcels in the Wehlener Sonnenuhr and Graacher sites. Traditional Mosel Riesling and a riverside cellar door.

Tasting: Tastings and sales by arrangement at the Maximinhof estate in Wehlen.

Weingut Kerpen ★ 4.4

A nine-generation family estate in Wehlen with parcels in the Wehlener Sonnenuhr, Graacher Domprobst and Bernkasteler sites. Riesling-focused, with a small amount of Spatburgunder.

Weingut Dr. Pauly-Bergweiler ★ 4.3

Mosel PradikatsweinRieslingPauly family (Helmut and Stefan Pauly)Bernkastel-Kues

A Bernkastel estate with holdings in the Bernkasteler alte Badstube am Doctorberg, Wehlener Sonnenuhr and Urziger Wurzgarten, plus the linked Peter Nicolay label. Classic fruity-style Mosel Riesling.

Tasting: Tastings and sales at the Bernkastel-Kues estate; visits by arrangement.

Gestade 15, 54470 Bernkastel-Kues, Germany → Book →

Weingut Wwe. Dr. H. Thanisch - Erben Muller-Burggraef ★ 4.3

Mosel PradikatsweinRieslingMuller-Burggraef heirsBernkastel-Kues

One of two Thanisch estates with historic parcels in the Bernkasteler Doctor, based in the Andel district of Bernkastel-Kues. Over 375 years of family winegrowing and classic Mosel Riesling.

Tasting: Open Monday to Friday 10:00 to 17:00 and Saturday 11:00 to 16:00, or by appointment, in Andel.

Junkerland 14, 54470 Bernkastel-Kues, Germany → Book →

Vineyards in Mosel, FAQ

When is the best time to visit Mosel for wine?

Peak wine-travel season in Mosel is spring through autumn, with harvest the standout window.

Do I need an appointment to taste at Mosel estates?

classified-growth and grand-cru estates require booking days to weeks ahead; smaller family domaines often take walk-ins midweek.

What hours do Mosel cellars and tasting rooms keep?

most estates open 10:00 to 17:00 by appointment, often closed Sunday and Monday.

How does tipping work at Mosel tastings?

tipping is not expected at tastings; buying a bottle from the cellar door is the customary thank-you.

What is the one wine to try in Mosel?

Ask the next local you meet what they would order. Mosel rewards trust.
