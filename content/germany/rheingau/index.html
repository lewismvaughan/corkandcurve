Rheingau Wine Guide

Riesling on quartzite and loess from Hochheim to Lorch, Spätburgunder on red slate at Assmannshausen

Updated May 2026 By Cork & Curve Editorial · Germany

The Rhine bend west of Wiesbaden where Riesling has been the singular grape since the 1720 monastic replanting at Schloss Johannisberg and Spätburgunder defines Assmannshausen.

Signature wines of Rheingau

The iconic bottles that define Rheingau.

Silberlack Riesling GG

Producer: schloss-johannisberg

The silver-foiled flagship dry Grosses Gewachs from the Johannisberg quartzite slope of the 1720 monastic estate.

Price: EUR 55 to 90 at retail

Goldlack Riesling Trockenbeerenauslese

Producer: schloss-johannisberg

The gold-foiled Schloss Johannisberg Trockenbeerenauslese, late-harvest botrytised Riesling in the 1775 Spaetlese lineage.

Price: EUR 400 to 900 at retail per 375ml

Gruenlack Riesling Spaetlese

Producer: schloss-johannisberg

Schloss Johannisberg's green-foiled fruity-sweet Spaetlese on the Johannisberg quartzite hill.

Price: EUR 35 to 55 at retail

Steinberger Riesling GG

Producer: hessische-staatsweingueter-kloster-eberbach

The walled-vineyard dry Grosses Gewachs from the 1136 Cistercian Steinberg above Eltville.

Price: EUR 45 to 70 at retail

Assmannshaeuser Hoellenberg Spaetburgunder GG

Producer: hessische-staatsweingueter-kloster-eberbach

The Hessian state estate's flagship dry Grosses Gewachs Spaetburgunder from the historic German Pinot enclave on red slate.

Price: EUR 50 to 85 at retail

Erbacher Marcobrunn Riesling GG

Producer: hessische-staatsweingueter-kloster-eberbach

The Hessian state estate's dry Grosses Gewachs Riesling from the Marcobrunn springwater vineyard.

Price: EUR 50 to 80 at retail

Vineyards to know in Rheingau

A handful of the estates we send friends to when they are in Rheingau.

Schloss Johannisberg

VDP.Grosse Lage RheingauRieslingSchloss Johannisberg, 65366 Geisenheim-Johannisberg, Germany

Hilltop Schloss Johannisberg above Geisenheim documents a 1720 Riesling replanting and a 1775 Spatlese courier story; 51.5 ha of Riesling-only quartzite.

More about Schloss Johannisberg →

Hessische Staatsweinguter Kloster Eberbach

VDP.Grosse Lage RheingauRiesling, Spatburgunder (Pinot Noir), Weissburgunder (Pinot Blanc), Grauburgunder (Pinot Gris)Kloster Eberbach, 65346 Eltville am Rhein, Germany

Hessian state wine estate at the 1136 Cistercian abbey of Eberbach, working 168 ha across the Rheingau with the walled Steinberg monopole and Assmannshausen.

More about Hessische Staatsweinguter Kloster Eberbach →

Schloss Vollrads

VDP.Grosse Lage RheingauRieslingSchloss Vollrads, 65375 Oestrich-Winkel, Germany

An 800-year-old Riesling-only estate above Winkel, with Greiffenclau wine sales to the Mainz St. Viktorstift from 1211; 68 ha planted entirely to Riesling.

More about Schloss Vollrads →

Weingut Robert Weil

VDP.Grosse Lage RheingauRieslingMuhlberg 5, 65399 Kiedrich, Germany

An 1875-founded Kiedrich Riesling estate run by Wilhelm Weil with Suntory as majority partner from 1988; 78 ha all Riesling, flagship Kiedricher Graefenberg.

More about Weingut Robert Weil →

Weingut Kunstler

VDP.Grosse Lage RheingauRiesling, Spatburgunder (Pinot Noir), Chardonnay, Sauvignon BlancGeheimrat-Hummel-Platz 1a, 65239 Hochheim, Germany

Gunter Kunstler's Hochheim estate, family-owned since 1648 and VDP member since 1994, working 68 ha across Hochheimer Holle, Domdechaney, Kirchenstuck and Stielweg.

More about Weingut Kunstler →

Weingut Leitz

VDP.Grosse Lage RheingauRiesling, Spatburgunder (Pinot Noir)Theodor-Heuss-Strasse 5, 65385 Rudesheim am Rhein, Germany

Johannes Leitz's Rudesheim estate, 111 ha of mostly Riesling across Berg Schlossberg, Berg Rottland, Berg Roseneck and Berg Kaisersteinfels.

Sub-appellations of Rheingau

Hochheim am Main (Hochheim/Hochheim am Main/Hochheimer)

Easternmost Rheingau village on the Main where 'hock' was born, with Victorian royal pedigree and Hochheimer Hölle on chalky loess.

Also: Hochheim am Main

Walluf (Walluf/Wallufer/Niederwalluf)

A compact Riesling commune on the route, with Walkenberg dry Rieslings from a 19th-century family estate next to the Rhine.

Eltville am Rhein (Eltville/Eltville am Rhein/Eltviller)

Sandstone riverside castle, June rose gardens and the headquarters of Rheingau wine, with Kloster Eberbach 15 minutes uphill.

Erbach (Erbach/Eltville-Erbach/Erbacher)

The Marcobrunn boundary stone, the riverside Schloss Reinhartshausen and a cluster of historic estates around the Erbacher church.

Also: Eltville-Erbach

Kiedrich (Kiedrich/Kiedricher/Kiedricher Gräfenberg)

Hilltop Gothic basilica, the Gräfenberg on phyllite slopes and the Robert Weil estate at the centre of the village.

Rauenthal (Rauenthal/Rauenthaler/Rauenthaler Baiken)

Hilltop forest-edge village with Baiken and Rothenberg on phyllite, perfumed cool-air Rieslings prized in 19th-century price lists.

Rheingau wine, FAQ

When is the best time to visit Rheingau for wine?

Peak wine-travel season in Rheingau is spring through autumn, with harvest the standout window.

Do I need an appointment to taste at Rheingau estates?

classified-growth and grand-cru estates require booking days to weeks ahead; smaller family domaines often take walk-ins midweek.

What hours do Rheingau cellars and tasting rooms keep?

most estates open 10:00 to 17:00 by appointment, often closed Sunday and Monday.

How does tipping work at Rheingau tastings?

tipping is not expected at tastings; buying a bottle from the cellar door is the customary thank-you.

What is the one wine to try in Rheingau?

If you only open one bottle, open Silberlack Riesling GG by schloss-johannisberg. It is the wine most associated with Rheingau.
