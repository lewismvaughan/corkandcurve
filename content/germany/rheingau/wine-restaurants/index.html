Cellar and plate in Rheingau

Ente ★ 4.7

Refined modern cuisine drawing on global flavours; the wine list anchors in Rheingau Rieslings with deep producer relationshipsPfortenhaus Kloster Eberbach, Eltville am Rhein

Hommage Hotels' Ente moved in March 2026 to the gatehouse of Kloster Eberbach during the Nassauer Hof renovation in Wiesbaden. Chef Michael Kammermeier's refined cooking with a Riesling-led list.

Awards: Michelin (one star) 2025

Kloster-Eberbach-Strasse 1, 65346 Eltville am Rhein, Germany →

Burgrestaurant Schwarzenstein ★ 4.4

Classical German cooking on the Burg with a wine list led by Rheingau VDP estatesJohannisberg, Geisenheim

Castle restaurant at Relais and Chateaux Burg Schwarzenstein above Geisenheim-Johannisberg. Classical German cooking and a Rheingau-anchored wine list with vineyard-view terrace seating.

Rosengasse 32, 65366 Geisenheim-Johannisberg, Germany →

Brasserie Schwarzenstein ★ 4.3

Authentic French brasserie cuisine by Christofer Kokozska and Marco Stenger in a glazed pavilion with Rhine valley views; Rheingau-led wine listJohannisberg, Geisenheim

Glazed-pavilion brasserie at Burg Schwarzenstein in Geisenheim-Johannisberg, French cuisine by Christofer Kokozska and Marco Stenger. Rheingau-led wine list with vineyard panoramas over the Rhine valley.

Rosengasse 32, 65366 Geisenheim-Johannisberg, Germany →

Restaurant Jean ★ 4.6

French-inspired fine dining by chef-patron Johannes Frankenbach with a wine list led by Rheingau growersEltville am Rhein

Eltville fine-dining destination of chef-patron Johannes Frankenbach with French-inspired tasting menus. The wine programme is led by Rheingau estates, supported by Bordeaux and Burgundy depth.

Awards: Michelin (one star) 2025

Wilhelmstrasse 13, 65343 Eltville am Rhein, Germany →

Restaurant Kronenschloesschen ★ 4.6

Modern French tasting menus by chef Marcus Langer, with the Gault Millau Beste Weinkarte Deutschlands wine list led by Head Sommelier Florian RichterHattenheim, Eltville am Rhein

Hattenheim fine-dining restaurant at the family-run 4-star Kronenschloesschen. Marcus Langer heads the kitchen since August 2025; sommelier Florian Richter, Germany's Best Sommelier 2023, runs the cellar.

Awards: Gault Millau Best Wine List in Germany 2024

Rheinallee, 65347 Eltville-Hattenheim, Germany →

Weinhaus Zum Krug ★ 4.4

350 bottlesTraditional Rheingau cooking with a 350-bottle wine list spanning Rheingau growers; sauerbraten, Wispertal trout and Rheingauer sausageHattenheim, Eltville am Rhein

Hotel and wine house in a 1720 half-timbered building in central Hattenheim, run by the Laufer family for three generations. Around 350 Rheingau wines alongside regional plates from grandmothers' recipes.

Hauptstrasse 34, 65347 Eltville-Hattenheim, Germany →

Klosterschaenke Kloster Eberbach ★ 4.3

Monastery tavern cooking with regional Hessian and Cistercian-tradition plates; wine list led entirely by the Hessische Staatsweinguter Kloster Eberbach rangeKloster Eberbach, Eltville

Monastery tavern at the 12th-century Kloster Eberbach, the Cistercian abbey above Eltville. The list is entirely Kloster Eberbach wines from the 200-hectare estate run by the Hessische Staatsweinguter.

Kloster Eberbach, 65346 Eltville am Rhein, Germany →

Schlossschaenke auf dem Johannisberg ★ 4.4

Seasonal regional cooking with Mediterranean accents; the wine list is built around Schloss Johannisberg's own RieslingsJohannisberg, Geisenheim

Hilltop restaurant of Schloss Johannisberg above Geisenheim with conservatory and sun-terrace seating. Set menus and a la carte built around the estate's own Riesling range with views over the Rheingau.

Schloss Johannisberg, 65366 Geisenheim-Johannisberg, Germany →

Marcobrunn by Tristan Brandt ★ 4.4

Restored gourmet restaurant of Hotel Schloss Reinhartshausen in Erbach, reopened September 2025 with chefs Simone Kubitzek and Hella Eggers under Tristan Brandt. Vignette Collection by IHG.

Hauptstrasse 41, 65346 Eltville-Erbach, Germany →

Restaurant Baiken by Schroeer ★ 4.4

Classical fine-dining cooking by Dirk Schroeer with the Rauenthal Baiken vineyard alongside; wine list led by Rheingau RieslingEltville Rauenthal

Restaurant in the Baiken vineyard pavilion above Rauenthal, run by Dirk Schroeer and Amila Begic-Schroeer. Classical fine-dining cooking with views over the Rheingau slopes and a Rheingau-led wine list.

Wiesweg 86, 65343 Eltville am Rhein, Germany →

Restaurant Lindenwirt ★ 4.1

Regional Rheingau cooking with a Drosselgasse wine garden; Schwaebisch Hall pasture-raised beef and local game alongside Rheingau RieslingsDrosselgasse, Ruedesheim am Rhein

Restaurant of the 1927 Hotel Lindenwirt on the Drosselgasse, run by the third generation of the Ohlig family. Regional cooking with Rheingau wines in the wine garden or the Wirtshaus zum Drosselmueller alongside.

Drosselgasse 4, 65385 Ruedesheim am Rhein, Germany →

Ruedesheimer Schloss Restaurant ★ 4.0

Regional Rheingau cooking in a 1729 tithe-barn courtyard; Rheingau Riesling and Assmannshaeuser Spatburgunder on the listDrosselgasse, Ruedesheim am Rhein

Restaurant at the Ruedesheimer Schloss on the corner of Steingasse and Drosselgasse, set in a 1729 former tithe barn. Regional Rheingau plates with the estate's Rieslings and the local Spatburgunder.

Steingasse 10, 65385 Ruedesheim am Rhein, Germany →

Heiliggeist Restaurant Mainz ★ 4.1

Restaurant in the Gothic former Heiliggeist hospital in Mainz old town, with vaulted ceilings and stained glass. Rheingau, Rheinhessen and Pfalz wines paired with seasonal regional plates.

Mailandsgasse 11, 55116 Mainz, Germany →

Weinhaus Sinz Restaurant ★ 4.0

Seasonal regional cooking in a half-century-old family wine house with Rheingau wines in the middle of the Wiesbaden vineyardsFrauenstein, Wiesbaden

Family-run wine-house restaurant in Wiesbaden-Frauenstein in the middle of the Rheingau vineyards. Seasonal regional cooking with Wiener Schnitzel, Tafelspitz, steaks and the house Rheingau wines.

Herrnbergstrasse 17, 65201 Wiesbaden, Germany →

Wine Restaurants in Rheingau, FAQ

When is the best time to visit Rheingau for wine?

Peak wine-travel season in Rheingau is spring through autumn, with harvest the standout window.

Do I need an appointment to taste at Rheingau estates?

classified-growth and grand-cru estates require booking days to weeks ahead; smaller family domaines often take walk-ins midweek.

What hours do Rheingau cellars and tasting rooms keep?

most estates open 10:00 to 17:00 by appointment, often closed Sunday and Monday.

How does tipping work at Rheingau tastings?

tipping is not expected at tastings; buying a bottle from the cellar door is the customary thank-you.

What is the one wine to try in Rheingau?

Ask the next local you meet what they would order. Rheingau rewards trust.
